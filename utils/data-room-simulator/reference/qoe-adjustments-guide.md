# Quality of Earnings Adjustments Guide

Common QoE adjustments by industry for M&A due diligence.

## Overview

Quality of Earnings (QoE) analysis identifies adjustments to reported EBITDA to arrive at a normalized, sustainable earnings figure. Adjustments fall into three categories:

1. **Non-recurring items** - One-time events not expected to continue
2. **Owner add-backs** - Personal expenses or above-market compensation
3. **Pro forma adjustments** - Run-rate changes (new contracts, cost savings)

---

## Universal Adjustments

### Non-Recurring Items

| Adjustment | Description | Typical Range |
|------------|-------------|---------------|
| Legal settlements | Lawsuit settlements, legal fees | $50K - $500K+ |
| Restructuring | Severance, facility closure | 1-5% of revenue |
| Insurance claims | One-time recoveries | Variable |
| M&A costs | Transaction-related fees | $50K - $200K |
| Write-offs | Bad debt, inventory obsolescence | 0.5-2% of revenue |

### Owner/Seller Adjustments

| Adjustment | Description | Typical Range |
|------------|-------------|---------------|
| Owner compensation | Salary above market rate | $100K - $500K |
| Owner benefits | Personal insurance, vehicles | $25K - $100K |
| Related party rent | Above-market rent to owner LLC | $50K - $200K |
| Family payroll | Non-working family members | $50K - $150K |
| Personal expenses | Travel, entertainment | $10K - $50K |

## SaaS-Specific Adjustments

### Revenue Adjustments

| Adjustment | Description | Impact |
|------------|-------------|--------|
| One-time implementation | Large setup fees recognized upfront | Negative |
| Multi-year prepayments | Unusual annual billing | Timing |
| Non-recurring revenue | One-time development work | Negative |

### Expense Adjustments

| Adjustment | Description | Impact |
|------------|-------------|--------|
| Stock-based comp | Non-cash expense add-back | Positive |
| Capitalized R&D | R&D vs. development allocation | Timing |
| Customer acquisition | One-time sales costs | Positive |
| Infrastructure migration | Cloud migration costs | Positive |

### Key Questions
- Is customer concentration improving or worsening?
- What's the trend in net revenue retention?
- Are implementation fees recurring or one-time?
- How is R&D capitalization handled?

## Red Flags to Watch For

### Revenue Quality
- [ ] High customer concentration (top 10 > 50%)
- [ ] Declining recurring revenue
- [ ] Unusual quarter-end acceleration
- [ ] Large one-time transactions
- [ ] Channel stuffing indicators

### Expense Quality
- [ ] Deferred maintenance
- [ ] Understaffing
- [ ] Below-market compensation
- [ ] Unusual related party transactions
- [ ] Aggressive capitalization policies

### Working Capital
- [ ] DSO increasing trend
- [ ] Inventory build-up
- [ ] Deferred revenue decline
- [ ] Accrual reversals

---

## Adjustment Documentation

For each adjustment, document:
1. **Description** - What the adjustment is
2. **Amount** - Dollar impact
3. **Support** - Source documentation
4. **Recurrence** - Why it won't continue
5. **Buyer view** - Will buyer agree?
