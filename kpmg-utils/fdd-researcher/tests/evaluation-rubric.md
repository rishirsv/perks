# FDD Researcher — Evaluation Rubric

Use this rubric to score the quality of research briefs generated by the FDD Researcher GPT.

---

## Scoring Summary

| Category | Weight | Max Score |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| **A. Structure & Format** | 15% | 15 |
| **B. CIM/Document Extraction** | 20% | 20 |
| **C. Industry Context Depth** | 25% | 25 |
| **D. Competitive Landscape** | 15% | 15 |
| **E. Actionability** | 15% | 15 |
| **F. Writing Quality** | 10% | 10 |
| **TOTAL** | 100% | 100 |

**Passing Score:** 70/100
**Target Score:** 85/100

---

## Category A: Structure & Format (15 points)

### A1. Section Completeness (5 points)

All 10 required sections present:
1. At-a-Glance
2. Company Overview
3. Financial Summary
4. Key Findings
5. Gaps & Unknowns
6. Industry Context
7. Diligence Playbook
8. Data Requests (P1/P2)
9. Management Questions
10. Sources

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | All 10 sections present |
| 4 | 9 sections present |
| 3 | 8 sections present |
| 2 | 6-7 sections present |
| 0-1 | <6 sections present |

### A2. No Process Artifacts (5 points)

Final brief should NOT contain:
- "Intake Summary" or document skim notes
- "Quick Questions" section
- "Research Plan" or "what I searched for"
- Process narration ("Let me now...")

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | No process artifacts |
| 3 | Minor process language (1-2 instances) |
| 0 | Process sections included in final output |

### A3. Length Appropriateness (5 points)

Target: 5 pages / 2,000-2,500 words

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | 1,800-2,800 words (appropriate depth) |
| 3 | 1,200-1,800 or 2,800-3,500 words |
| 1 | <1,200 words (too shallow) |
| 0 | >3,500 words (too verbose) |

---

## Category B: CIM/Document Extraction (20 points)

*For public company tests, evaluate based on 10-K/S-1 extraction quality*

### B1. Financial Data Extraction (5 points)

Did the brief extract key financials?
- Revenue (with growth rates)
- Margins (gross, EBITDA/operating)
- Revenue composition/mix
- Key segment breakdown

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | All key financials with source attribution |
| 3 | Most financials, some gaps |
| 1 | Minimal financial extraction |

### B2. Business Model Extraction (5 points)

Did the brief capture how the company makes money?
- Pricing model / unit economics
- Customer segments
- Contract structure (if applicable)
- Revenue streams breakdown

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Clear, detailed business model explanation |
| 3 | Basic business model, missing details |
| 1 | Vague or generic description |

### B3. Quality Signal Extraction (5 points)

Did the brief identify FDD-critical items?
- Customer concentration (or note if not disclosed)
- Add-backs / adjustments (if applicable)
- Organic vs acquired growth
- Recurring vs non-recurring mix

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Quality signals identified and flagged |
| 3 | Some quality signals, missed others |
| 1 | No quality signals identified |

### B4. Marketing Fluff Avoidance (5 points)

Did the brief avoid treating marketing content as fact?
- No TAM/SAM cited as fact
- No "market leader" claims taken at face value
- Projections flagged as unvalidated

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Clean separation of fact vs marketing |
| 3 | Some marketing content included uncritically |
| 0 | Heavy reliance on marketing claims |

---

## Category C: Industry Context Depth (25 points)

*This is the highest-weighted category — Industry Context should teach a generalist how the business works*

### C1. Business Model Mechanics (8 points)

Does the brief explain HOW the company makes money, step by step?

For insurance brokerage:
- Commission economics (rates, splits, carrier relationships)
- Contingent profit commissions (what triggers them)
- Organic growth drivers (new business, cross-sell, rate cycle)
- Producer economics

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 8 | Detailed mechanics explanation; generalist could explain to colleague |
| 6 | Good mechanics, missing 1-2 key elements |
| 4 | Basic mechanics, surface-level |
| 2 | Minimal mechanics explanation |
| 0 | No mechanics — just surface description |

### C2. Benchmarks with Sources (7 points)

Does the brief provide industry benchmarks with citations?
- Margin benchmarks (gross, EBITDA)
- Growth rate norms
- Industry-specific KPIs (NRR for SaaS, combined ratio for insurance, etc.)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 7 | 3+ benchmarks with credible sources cited |
| 5 | 2-3 benchmarks with sources |
| 3 | Benchmarks mentioned but no sources |
| 1 | Vague benchmark references |
| 0 | No benchmarks |

### C3. Word Count Check (5 points)

Industry Context should be 400-500 words.

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | 400+ words, appropriate depth |
| 3 | 300-400 words, adequate |
| 1 | 200-300 words, too shallow |
| 0 | <200 words |

### C4. Industry Dynamics (5 points)

Does the brief explain what's happening in the industry?
- M&A trends
- Regulatory changes
- Technology shifts
- Market cycle effects

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Current dynamics with specific examples |
| 3 | Some dynamics mentioned, generic |
| 1 | No industry dynamics |

---

## Category D: Competitive Landscape (15 points)

### D1. Competitor Count (5 points)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | 5+ named competitors with context |
| 4 | 4 competitors |
| 3 | 3 competitors |
| 2 | 2 competitors |
| 1 | 1 competitor |
| 0 | No competitors named |

### D2. Competitor Context (5 points)

For each competitor, is there meaningful context?
- Approximate scale (revenue, employees, market position)
- Strategic focus or differentiation
- Recent activity (M&A, funding, IPO)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Rich context for each competitor |
| 3 | Basic context (just names and scale) |
| 1 | Names only, no context |

### D3. M&A Market Context (5 points)

For active M&A sectors, does the brief include:
- Deal volume / activity level
- Valuation multiples (with ranges)
- Deal structure norms (earnouts, rollover)
- Key acquirers

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Comprehensive M&A context with multiples |
| 3 | Some M&A context, missing multiples |
| 1 | Mentioned M&A exists, no detail |
| 0 | No M&A context |

---

## Category E: Actionability (15 points)

### E1. Data Requests Quality (5 points)

Are data requests specific and prioritized?
- P1 vs P2 distinction
- Period/format specified
- Rationale for each request
- Tied to Key Findings/Gaps

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Specific, prioritized, with rationale |
| 3 | Good requests, missing some detail |
| 1 | Generic requests |

### E2. Management Questions Quality (5 points)

Are management questions insightful?
- Tied to specific risks/gaps
- Include "what we're testing" context
- Not generic (avoid "tell us about your business")

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Insightful questions with testing rationale |
| 3 | Good questions, some generic |
| 1 | Mostly generic questions |

### E3. Diligence Playbook Quality (5 points)

Does the playbook provide clear guidance?
- Value driver tree present
- Specific workstreams with "what to test / how / risk"
- Tied to Key Findings

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Clear playbook with value driver tree |
| 3 | Good playbook, no tree |
| 1 | Generic diligence guidance |

---

## Category F: Writing Quality (10 points)

### F1. Density (5 points)

Is the writing dense and efficient?
- Bullets over prose
- Tables for structured data
- Every sentence adds new information
- No throat-clearing or repetition

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Dense, efficient, no fluff |
| 3 | Mostly dense, some padding |
| 1 | Verbose, repetitive |

### F2. Specificity (5 points)

Is the writing specific?
- Numbers, names, dates (not vague)
- Concrete examples
- Source attribution

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 5 | Highly specific throughout |
| 3 | Mostly specific, some vague sections |
| 1 | Vague, generic language |
