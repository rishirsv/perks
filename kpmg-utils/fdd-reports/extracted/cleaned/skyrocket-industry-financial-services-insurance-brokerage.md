# Profit and Loss Overview / Financial Performance

## Overview

The summary adjusted income statement above allocates the results of the Company between core operations (main P&C and group benefits brokerage activities), CPCs and bonus commissions, and other items (largely comprising interest income generated on trust bank balances and on premium financing arrangements). Revenue and earnings performance over the Historical Period are underpinned by the following trends:

Notwithstanding revenue growth of 14.9% achieved over the Historical Period, pro forma adjusted EBITDA recorded modest growth of $0.9 million between FY22 and FY24, with increased operating costs weighing on earnings performance.

## Key Drivers

1. P&C base commissions: year-on-year growth in P&C base commissions as a result of increased production and policy pricing upside (CAGR: 9.0%);
2. base commissions in group benefits: stable base commissions in group benefits, with more limited annual increase between FY23 and FY24 (CAGR: 1.5%);
3. operating expenses: annual step-ups in operating expenses which trended broadly in line with revenue from FY22 to FY24, notably with respect to producer commissions (paid relative to revenue) and indirect headcount which increased by approximately 21% (+121 FTEs) between FY22 and FY24.
4. CPC and bonus commissions: moderate upside in CPC and bonus commissions between FY22 and FY24 driven by incremental volume, offset by annual variability in carrier loss ratios.
5. group benefits base commissions: Despite demonstrating year-on-year increase between FY22 and FY23 of 34.6% (mainly as a result of acquisitions), group benefits base commission income delivered limited annual growth in FY24 (+0.4%), partly as a result of client churn in some larger accounts.
6. base commission revenue associated with group benefits: We understand base commission revenue associated with group benefits is recognized at inception of the policy regardless of policy term, with any true-ups for participant coverage selections or membership population at the end of the policy term recognized out-of-period. Management indicated that the historical quantum of out-of-period commission true-ups associated with group benefits plans was not significant but could not provide supporting documentation to verify this assertion.
7. CPC commissions: CPC commissions are generated from the Company’s insurance carrier partners based on specific performance metrics which consider production volume, new business development, loss ratios on policies sold, among other factors. Overall, CPC revenues trended lower in FY23 relative to FY22, mainly as a result of higher losses incurred by carriers across the market which normalized through FY24.
8. CPC commissions: Management estimates and accrues CPC commissions in the P&C business through the calendar year, with collection in March / April of each subsequent period once computations are finalized by carriers. Revenue has historically been subject to periodic true-up during the fiscal year as visibility over the full year amount becomes available, with a final adjustment recognized at year end to align CPC revenue with amounts confirmed by carriers. CPC income recorded for FY24 reflects Management’s forecast bonuses for the 2024 risk year, with in excess of 85% confirmed with carriers at the time of reporting.
9. Group benefits bonus commissions: Group benefits bonus commissions exhibited more stable year-on-year growth through the Historical Period, underpinned by gains from incremental volume contributed in FY23 from previous year acquisitions, alongside a catch-up $0.2 million bonus in FY24 related to FY23.

Contingent and bonus commissions are accrued based on Management’s best estimates during the year and subject to periodic adjustment as additional information becomes available. ## Appendix 9: Basis of preparation

KPMG LLP (‘KPMG’) has been engaged by Client (Ares Capital Management LLC) to prepare a financial due diligence report in connection with the potential acquisition of Synex Benefits Solutions Inc. (‘Target’ or the ‘Company’). The report has been prepared in accordance with our engagement letter dated February 20, 2025.

Our work on historical financial performance comprises the fiscal years FY22, FY23, and FY24, and the trailing twelve month period ended October 31, 2024 (collectively, the “Historical Period”). We have analyzed the balance sheet as at December 31, 2023, December 31, 2024, and October 31, 2024 and our analysis of net working capital was performed on monthly balance sheet captions for the period January 2023 to December 2024.

Our scope of work for the current phase did not include analysis of the sales cube / book of business for the Historical Period.

Please refer to Appendix 8 for full details on the scope of our work.

During the course of our work our main points of contact have been representatives from the EY sell-side diligence team, including by Julie Marineau (Partner) and David Orloff (Senior Manager), and Management of Target, represented by Yan Charbonneau (CVO), Sylvie Lambert (CFO), Marc Noel (SVP of Finance), and Emile Sauvageau (VP of M&A and Finance).

We held an initial call with Management on February 20, 2025, and arranged a follow up call which took place on March 5, 2025.

Overall, we have had reasonable access to both the Management and the EY sell-side due diligence teams, which have been co-operative in providing various information requests and responding to our questions in a timely manner.

The contents of our report have not been reviewed by the Management of Target and the factual accuracy of this report has not been confirmed by the Management.

The requested information has been provided through the virtual data room to which we have had unrestricted access. As of the date of this report, certain information requests and questions remain outstanding with Management.

Our work has relied on certain key sources of information including, but not limited to; (i) EY sell-side due diligence report and  corresponding datapack covering periods up to October 31, 2024, (ii) EY sell-side due diligence roll-forward quality of earnings and net working capital datapacks prepared to December 31, 2024; (iii) consolidated audited financial statements for the Company for the years ended December 31, 2022 and December 31, 2023; (iv) various account breakdowns and supporting schedules provided by Management and EY. We note some areas where Management could not provide requested information which was fully reconcilable to the internal financial statements, specifically with respect to: (i) consolidated balance sheet listings at year end dates; (ii) analysis of base and bonus commission income for group benefits for all Historical Periods; and (iii) consolidated schedule of historical payroll costs for all periods in the scope of our work.
