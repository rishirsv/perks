# Executive Summary

- Adjusted net revenue PMPM increases over the historical period as a result of: (i) implementing the Company’s care management process for recent acquisitions, and (ii) increasing MRA over time due to age and better diagnosis of new members.
- Adjusted EBITDA PMPM increases over the historical period, primarily driven by revenue increases as the Company maintained margins through its revenue expansion. The Company’s primary expenses are payroll (35% of TTM Aug-20 net revenue) and MSO provider payments (15% of TTM Aug-20), both of which are revenue-driven and have remained consistent percentage-wise throughout the historical period.

# Key Findings

- Deferred payments ($-18.8 million): Deferred purchase price payments (in addition to what is included in reported net debt, associated with acquisitions should be considered net debt.
- De novo losses ($-13.8 million): EBITDA losses generated by de novo locations should be considered net debt given pro forma EBITDA adjustments have been applied assuming a steady positive EBITDA run-rate.

# Business Overview

- Cano Health, LLC (the “Company”) operates in healthcare industry providing patient centric primary care services to Medicare Advantage and Managed Medicaid members in Florida and Puerto Rico.
- The Company’s revenues are primarily from staff model and MSO capitation arrangements. It is supplemented with pharmacy, physiotherapy, and fee-for-service.
- In the TTM Aug-20 period, the Company completed three major acquisitions: Healthy Partners, Primary Care Physicians and Belen Medical Centers. These added approximately 54,000 members (at the time of closing).
