5. Not present in source report: As [ENTITY 1] has no allowance for doubtful debt policy, and we observe that there are long outstanding trade and other receivables balance of longer than one year. We propose to exclude long-outstanding receivables from net working capital due to being a non-current item in nature.
6. Not present in source report: According to [ENTITY 1]’s accounting policy, all issued invoices during the period will be recorded as revenue regardless of the period that the revenue should be recognized. Please refer to Appendix 2 for further details.
7. Not present in source report: Unearned revenue has both debt-like and working capital characteristics. The current portion of unearned revenue is currently treated as part of net working capital item with services due to be delivered within the next 12 months. With the nature of the school operation where invoices are usually sent ahead of the next academic year, unearned revenue balance is therefore a usual liability balance to appear on Balance sheet and part of working capital.
8. Not present in source report: We’ve proposed to exclude the non-current portion of unearned revenue from working capital as it relates to the service to be provided in more than 12 months. In the non-current balance, for the portion that the school has already received cash in advance for, we have treated this as a debt-like item as this represents the liability and obligation that the School has towards its students, and that while cash collected may have already been used by the school for working capital purpose, there's a risk of returning this cash, if students request for the refund in accordance with the school refund policy.
