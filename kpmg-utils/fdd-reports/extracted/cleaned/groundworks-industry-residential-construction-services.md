# Executive Summary

- Pro forma adjusted EBITDA [4] increased by $38.0 million from 2020 to LTM Jun-22, predominantly due to volume driven top-line growth.
- Pro forma EBITDA [4] margins declined over the same period, due to (i) inflation-driven material expense increases; (ii) rising fuel and
freight costs; (iii) increased insurance expense; (iv) marketing investments to drive sales; and (v) personnel investments to support growth;
partly offset by (vi) price increase, and (vii) leverage of corporate and branch infrastructure. Margins stabilized and increased in YTD Jun-
22 as compared to LTM Jun-22.

# Business Overview

- Founded in 2016 and headquartered in Virginia Beach, Virginia, Groundworks,
LLC and subsidiaries (“Groundworks”, or “the Company”) provide foundation
repair and water management solutions primarily for residential customers. The
Company was acquired by the current majority owner, Cortec Group, in Jan-20
and has since grown significantly through a combination of organic growth and
acquisitions (2020 (5), 2021 (5) and YTD Jun-22 (3)).
- The Company is currently organized and managed by using a divisional
operating structure, further segregated by region, based on geographic location
of branches and their geographic service areas. The Company’s corporate
office, executive leadership team, and corporate functions are predominantly
centralized within the Virginia Beach headquarters.
The current operating structure presented below was established in 2021 as
the Company expanded geographically.
- The Company operates in the US (with no international operations) and
sources materials used in its services primarily from domestic sources, with de
minimis international vendor relationships. Operations are transacted in USD.

The Company’s operations and revenue reporting are largely centered around
what are referred to internally as “jobs”. Jobs represent the main contract
between the Company and the customer, and can be made up of multiple
underlying “projects”. Jobs are typically short term (~1-3 days) and the
completion of all projects on a job is the driver of revenue recognition. Refer to
the Appendix – Basis of presentation for further detail on how the Company
recognizes revenue.

The Company offers third party consumer financing primarily through
GreenSky, with ~29% of gross revenue financed by customers. Acquisitions
may sometimes continue with historical financing programs for a short period
post-acquisition, however the majority either already utilize GreenSky or
transfer to GreenSky upon acquisition.
