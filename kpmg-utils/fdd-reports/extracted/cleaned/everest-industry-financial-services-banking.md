PCFIA acts as an agency, distributing home and auto insurance policies. PCFIB operates as a multi-carrier broker, placing policies with 10+ insurers. It earns 7.5% net commission on gross written premiums, with supplemental income from insurer partnerships. Neither holds underwriting risk or ownership ties to insurers.
- PCB takes part in the PC Optimum Loyalty Points Program, where customers can earn points on credit card transactions (at all LCL stores and elsewhere).
- The primary offerings include the following:
- PC Mastercard (PCMC): PCB specializes in rewards-driven financial products, leveraging LCL’s PC Optimum member-base to cross-sell credit cards. The primary offerings are the PC Mastercard, PC Silver Mastercard, PC World Mastercard, and PC Insiders™ World Elite Mastercard.
- PC Money Account (PCMA): The PC Money Account is a no-fee digital account, integrated with the PC Optimum Loyalty Points Program. Customers can earn PC Optimum points for everyday banking activities, including spend. In FY24, there were 168,491 new PCMA’s opened. In Oct-24, PCB launched a new savings option with a current 3.5% high-yield savings account.

5. Change in breakage rates: Under IFRS 15 (Revenue from Contracts with Customers), companies must: (a) Proportionate the loyalty points issued, and recognize income only upon redemption/expiry of the loyalty points. At the time of sale, a portion of the revenue associated with future redemptions is recorded as a deferred revenue liability to be recognized when the redemption occurs at a future time. Companies must estimate a breakage rate (a percentage of points never redeemed) to allocate revenue between current recognition and a future liability. The breakage rate is the inverse of the ultimate redemption rate (URR).
   As a result of elevated in-year redemption rates since 2023, the loyalty liability has declined. Accordingly, at Dec-24, Management reassessed the current URR of 92.3% to ensure the loyalty liability is appropriately funded based on recent trends. Management utilized three established and industry-recognized statistical models, leveraging historical redemption data, to determine a revised URR of 93.5% which resulted in a $29.2 million adjustment to the loyalty liability. This adjustment removes the one-time catch up for the change in rates at Dec-24 and applies the impact to the historical period assuming a URR of 93.5%.
   Refer to Appendix 8.2 for additional supporting details.
