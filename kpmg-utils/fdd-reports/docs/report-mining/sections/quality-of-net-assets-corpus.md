### Verbatim Extract


- The balance sheet information presented herein begin at Everest (i.e. includes all entities within the transaction). Refer to the Basis of presentation section for further details on the reconciliation from Legal entity to Everest.
- Everest’s fiscal year is a 12 month period ending on December 31st, with three full periods presented in the Quality of net assets. The Quality of net assets analysis is presented on an adjusted basis and does not include standalone considerations. The trial balances and ancillary schedules are utilized to build up the Quality of net assets analysis for FY22, FY23 and FY24 (refer to Basis of preparation and Basis of presentation for further details on the sources of financial information).
- Such proposed adjustments are not deemed to be all inclusive and are presented on an indicative basis. The validity of these potential adjustments and other factors raised for your consideration may require further investigation to understand the potential impact to your assessment of Everest.
- The nature of the adjustments included in the analysis to derive Net asset value (“NAV”) are as follows:
- NAV, reported: Represents total assets less liabilities (net asset value) per Everest’s Balance sheet. Everest was used as the foundation of the NAV analysis as this represents all entities in the scope of the proposed transaction perimeter. Refer to the Basis of presentation for further details on the reconciliation from Legal Entity to Everest.
- NAV, adjusted: Represents the NAV following adjustments identified as part of the due diligence process, including one-time items which are not part of recurring operations.
- Other considerations: Additional items that are subjective in nature and/or not quantifiable, are included in the ‘Other considerations’ section.
- See the following pages for additional details.
- Note: The average columns are calculated based on the monthly average balance in each fiscal year.
- As of Dec-24, Everest’s reported net assets are $1.3 billion, and the adjusted net assets are $1.0 billion.
- The following are adjustments identified during the due diligence process.
- Remove PSI cash balance: PSI is operated as a separate legal entity that consolidates results from unrelated entities (PCFIA and PCFIB), outside of Everest. PSI’s reported cash balance reflects the build-up of historical operations and is in excess of the required cash for operations.
- Post-transaction, the excess cash will not transfer with PSI and has been removed from the net asset value. Management represents that the current operating level of cash is approximately $2 million. This adjustment removes PSI’s reported cash balance, and includes the indicative cash required for daily operations.
- Notational input tax credits: Everest generates NITC balances in relation to the PC Optimum Loyalty Points Program. Consistent with the Quality of earnings (adjustment #4), as a result of legislative uncertainty relating to the future state of NITC’s, this adjustment is applied to remove the associated receivable and provision from net assets. The adjusted amounts are based on an indicative approach based on information on cash receipt and payment obtained from discussions with Management.
- Change in breakage rates: Consistent with the Quality of earnings adjustment #5, this adjustment reflects the current URR of 93.5% (previously 92.3%) retroactively from the beginning of the analysis period for normalization purposes (refer to Appendix 8.2 for further details on the adjustment).
- Mastercard GST: As a result of the Canadian Revenue Agency (CRA) announcement in Jun-23 that it would retroactively collect GST from Financial Institutions on payment card network operator services paid to Mastercard from 2013 to Q2 2023, as discussed within the Quality of earnings (adjustment #12), Everest recognized a payable to the CRA. In Dec-23, the CRA announced the retroactive collection period would be amended from 2013 onwards to 2016 onwards and a subsequent reversal of the provision was made. During FY24, Everest has paid a portion of the GST payable. Management represents that as at Dec-24, there is a $9 million payable remaining. As such, the associated provision and release are removed from net assets, using an indicative approach based on information on cash receipt and payment obtained from discussions with Management.
- Balances owing to/from LCL: Everest has balances owing to and from LCL related to PC Optimum Loyalty Points Program and expenses, accrued payroll, and shared cost allocations (refer to Appendix 8.4 for a detailed breakdown). The following adjustments are considered for the Quality of net assets:
- Remove balances owing to/from LCL: An adjustment is made to remove the reported balances owing to/from LCL from net assets.
- Post-transaction payable considerations: There are certain balances within the intercompany accounts which relate to activities that will continue post-transaction, including loyalty program payables and payroll accruals. Post-transaction, Everest will have payables associated with these activities, which should be included in adjusted net assets. An adjustment is made to reflect the indicative post-transaction payable balances for loyalty program costs and indicative payroll accruals.
- Mastercard incentive benefit: As discussed in QofE adjustment #6, Mastercard paid $27.5 million in cash reimbursement to PCB in 2024 as part of the incentive benefit. PCB recognized $22.5 million of the incentive benefits into income and deferred 50% of the $10 million Ethoca incentive to 2025. As at Dec-24, Everest has deferred revenue of $5 million recorded. Consistent with the Quality of earnings analysis, incentive benefit is considered non-recurring, and an adjustment is made to remove the deferred revenue balance from the quality of earnings.
- Standalone considerations: There are potential incremental FTE and non-FTE expenses relating to standalone considerations which may result in associated payables and accruals. Consideration may be made on the impact of the incremental standalone costs to the net assets.
- GRE insurance contract liabilities: Insurance contract liabilities are recognized when contracts are entered and premiums are charged. These liabilities are based on the cost of all claims reported or incurred but not settled at the settlement of financial position date together with reported claims handling costs and reduction for the expected value of salvage. At the time of this report, we have not yet reviewed an actuarial report supporting the valuation of these liabilities.
