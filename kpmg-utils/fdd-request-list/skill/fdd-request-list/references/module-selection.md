# Module Selection

Select modules based on engagement facts, not as a flat checklist.

## Always include

Include the core backbone for every workbook:
- Highest priority
- High priority
- Medium priority
- Low priority

## Include when the facts call for it

### Sell-side report available

Include when a vendor diligence report or third-party databook already exists.

Use to request:
- sell-side report
- supporting databook

### Audited or workpaper access

Include when audited statements exist and reconciliation to the audit is important.

Use to request:
- access to audit workpapers
- TB to audit mapping

### Carve-out

Include when the target is separated from a parent or shared platform.

Use to request:
- roll-up to parent audit
- shared cost allocations
- stand-alone costs
- one-time separation costs
- TSA or reverse TSA expectations
- shared assets
- org chart with shared functions

### Monthly reporting packages

Include when internal board packs or management packages can accelerate QoE and bridge work.

### Discontinued operations

Include when a product line or business unit is being shut down or excluded from run rate.

### Acquisitions

Include when acquired entities affect comparability across the historical period.

### Software or subscription

Include when recurring revenue, MRR, bookings, renewals, capitalization, or SaaS metrics matter.

### Manufacturing or inventory or BOM

Include when standard costing, BOMs, inventory aging, or warranty exposures drive QoE or working capital.

### Other balance sheet requests

Include when prepaid expenses, accruals, reserves, or other asset and liability buckets require targeted support.

### Energy

Include when detailed cash flow, projection bridges, hedging, cash receipts, or cash disbursements are needed.

### Regulated utility

Include when regulatory assets and liabilities, riders, rate base, or over or under earnings are central.

## Prioritization Guidance

- Put gating data in `Highest priority` even if the workbook example placed similar content elsewhere.
- Use `High priority` for high-value follow-on requests that are needed early but are not first-unlock blockers.
- Use conditional section titles exactly as visible workbook labels when you want those headings to appear in Excel.
- Keep `Other balance sheet requests` and `Other potential items to consider if material` as trailing modules unless the deal facts make them urgent.
