# FDD Writing Standards

## Preferred Terminology and Tone

Use precise, cautious diligence language. Prefer evidence, basis, and implications over advocacy.

| Imprecise or risky phrasing | FDD-preferred phrasing |
|-----------------|-------------|
| "a lot of growth" | "revenue increased by X% over [period]" |
| "pretty good margins" | "gross margin was X% in FY2025" |
| "stable working capital" | "working capital was within a range of X to Y days over the period reviewed" |
| "one-time item" | "management identified this as non-recurring; support reviewed was [basis]" |
| "normal EBITDA" | "adjusted EBITDA" or "QoE-adjusted EBITDA", if defined |
| "debt is low" | "net debt was $X as of [date]" |
| "big customer issue" | "customer concentration represented X% of revenue" |
| "strong business" | "the company demonstrated [specific evidenced attribute]" |
| "clean books" | "no material issues were identified in the areas reviewed" |
| "obvious add-back" | "the add-back appears supportable based on [basis]" |

## Core Writing Rules

- State the basis before the implication whenever possible.
- Distinguish clearly between reported results, management adjustments, and reviewer conclusions.
- Define the key earnings measure once and use that defined term consistently.
- Use explicit time periods and as-of dates.
- Quantify statements instead of using vague qualifiers.
- Mark uncertainty when support is incomplete.
- Use "appears", "based on information provided", or "management indicated" when the evidence does not support firmer language.
- Do not present preliminary observations as final conclusions.

## Language Patterns to Avoid

- **Casual phrasing**: "pretty good", "a bunch of", "huge", "no-brainer"
- **Overclaiming**: "proves", "clearly shows", "definitely", "fully supports" without strong evidence
- **Undefined metric labels**: switching between EBITDA, adjusted EBITDA, and QoE EBITDA without definition
- **Ambiguous ownership**: conclusions that do not distinguish management's view from the reviewer's view
- **Incomplete scope language**: conclusions stated without clarifying the period, entities, or sources reviewed

## Preferred FDD Phrasing Patterns

**Adjustment commentary**:
- "Management identified [item] as non-recurring; based on the support reviewed, the adjustment appears [supportable / partially supportable / unsupported]."
- "The adjustment increased EBITDA by $X and relates to [specific item] in [period]."
- "Further support is required before this item can be concluded as non-recurring."

**Trend commentary**:
- "Revenue increased from $X to $Y over the period reviewed, driven primarily by [driver]."
- "Margin decreased by X basis points, primarily due to [driver], based on management explanations and the schedules provided."

**Risk commentary**:
- "A potential diligence risk was identified in [area]; the available information does not yet quantify the full impact."
- "Based on the information provided, this issue may affect [earnings / working capital / net debt / reporting quality]."

**Limitation commentary**:
- "This conclusion is based on the information provided through [date]."
- "The analysis did not include [scope limitation], which may affect the conclusion."

## Reviewer Reminders

- Prefer narrowing a claim over polishing an unsupported claim.
- If a figure conflicts across sections, flag the conflict rather than choosing one silently.
- If a section is missing expected support, call it out as a coverage gap.
- If the report is clean, keep the verdict concise and avoid inventing optional improvements.
