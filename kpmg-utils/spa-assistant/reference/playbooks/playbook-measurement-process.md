# Playbook — Measurement & Process

## Statements package (Closing Statement; dispute process) [statements_package]

Plain-English meaning  
The statements package defines which statements are prepared after Closing, who prepares them, the timeline to object, and how disputes are resolved. It controls how the final purchase price is settled.

Typical / Buyer-leaning / Seller-leaning patterns

- Typical: draft/estimated statement, objection window, dispute notice, Independent Accountant as expert, final and binding.
- Buyer-leaning: longer review window, access to workpapers, accountant limited to disputed items, manifest-error carve-out.
- Seller-leaning: shorter window, broader accountant scope, more flexibility to adjust beyond disputed items.

Pitfalls + drafting tells

- Who prepares the statement is unclear, or timelines conflict with other mechanics.
- Independent Accountant can re-write the entire statement (not limited to disputed items).
- Accounting Principles/hierarchy not expressly applied in the dispute.
- “Final and binding” is used, but the scope is not constrained.

Counsel questions

- Who prepares each statement (Estimated/Draft/Final), and what support must the other party provide?
- What is the objection window, and is it realistic for diligence/testing?
- Is the Independent Accountant limited to disputed items and required to apply the Accounting Principles?
- How are “manifest error” corrections handled without reopening the entire statement?

Likely pushback

- “We need flexibility to correct errors and make the statement accurate.”
- “Limiting scope increases gamesmanship.”

Rebuttals / fallback

- “Use a disputed-items-only scope plus a manifest-error carve-out; it protects both sides.”
- “Access rights and clear accounting hierarchy reduce gamesmanship more than a broad scope does.”

Minimal edits

- The Independent Accountant shall resolve ~~the Closing Statement~~ **only those items set forth in the Dispute Notice**.
- In resolving disputes, the Independent Accountant shall apply the Accounting Principles~~.~~ **and shall not introduce new classifications or methods.**
- Seller shall have ~~10~~ **30** days to deliver a Dispute Notice.

## Accounting Principles / Hierarchy [accounting_principles_hierarchy]

Plain-English meaning  
Accounting Principles/hierarchy defines how the statements are prepared when GAAP/IFRS, past practice, and templates conflict. This is where many post-close disputes are won or lost.

Typical / Buyer-leaning / Seller-leaning patterns

- Typical: hierarchy specifying order of priority (Agreement → Schedules/Templates → Past Practice → GAAP/IFRS).
- Buyer-leaning: templates are controlling, plus no-hindsight and no-reclass protections.
- Seller-leaning: GAAP-only, or more discretion to correct classifications post-close.

Pitfalls + drafting tells

- No explicit hierarchy (GAAP vs past practice fight).
- Templates/schedules exist but are “illustrative” (not controlling).
- Reclassification between Cash/Debt/WC/Expenses is not prohibited.
- No-hindsight protection is missing.

Counsel questions

- What controls if GAAP conflicts with past practice or a sample schedule?
- Are sample statements controlling or illustrative?
- Is there a no-hindsight rule (facts as of cut-off only)?
- Is there a no-reclass rule across Cash/Indebtedness/WC/Expenses/Taxes?

Likely pushback

- “GAAP should control; templates can’t override GAAP.”

Rebuttals / fallback

- “If GAAP controls, then align the templates and remove conflicting examples; otherwise make the hierarchy explicit.”
- “No-hindsight and no-reclass protect both sides from post-close bucket shifting.”

Minimal edits

- The Closing Statement shall be prepared in accordance with ~~GAAP~~ **the following order of priority: (i) this Agreement, (ii) the Sample Statements, (iii) past practice, and (iv) GAAP**.
- No item shall be reclassified between Cash, Indebtedness, Net Working Capital and Transaction Expenses~~.~~ **, except to correct manifest error.**
- The Closing Statement shall be prepared ~~with the benefit of information available after Closing~~ **without hindsight, based solely on facts existing as of the Cut-off Time**.

## Anti-duplication constructs (without duplication) [anti_duplication]

Plain-English meaning  
Anti-duplication language prevents the same item from being counted twice across overlapping buckets (Cash, Indebtedness, Net Working Capital, Transaction Expenses, Taxes). It is a small drafting addition that prevents large disputes.

Typical / Buyer-leaning / Seller-leaning patterns

- Typical: formula-level “without duplication” plus “to the extent included” carve-outs in definitions.
- Buyer-leaning: formula-level rule + routing for common overlap items (overdrafts, restricted cash, taxes, deal costs, intercompany).
- Seller-leaning: anti-dup only inside definitions or left to the Independent Accountant.

Pitfalls + drafting tells

- Anti-dup exists only inside one definition (e.g., Cash) but not at the formula level.
- Overlaps are not enumerated or routed; parties assume the accountant will “fix it.”
- Dispute process does not authorize the expert to resolve overlap classification.

Counsel questions

- Is “without duplication” applied at the **formula level** (not only inside definitions)?
- Are overlaps routed (Taxes vs WC, Transaction Expenses vs Debt/WC, overdrafts, intercompany)?
- Does the Independent Accountant have a mandate to enforce the no-duplication rule?

Likely pushback

- “Anti-dup is implied; we don’t need more drafting.”

Rebuttals / fallback

- “Implied anti-dup is litigated; one sentence prevents double recovery and reduces expert disputes.”
- “If you prefer, keep it as a short formula-level sentence plus targeted routing for known overlaps.”

Minimal edits

- All calculations of Cash, Indebtedness, Net Working Capital, Transaction Expenses and Taxes shall be made ~~as set forth herein~~ **on a basis without duplication**.
- For avoidance of doubt, any item included in Indebtedness shall ~~also be included in Net Working Capital~~ **not be included in Net Working Capital, Transaction Expenses or Taxes**.
- The Independent Accountant shall not make any determination that results in ~~double counting~~ **any item being included more than once across the adjustment calculations**.

SPA evidence anchors

- Project Eleos — Definitions (Cash includes “without duplication” concept).
- Project Skyrocket — Definitions (Net Debt formulation includes “without duplication” and includes Transaction Expenses/Unpaid Transaction Expenses).
- Multiple agreements — scattered “to the extent included” carve-outs across definitions.

## Cut-off / Measurement times [cutoff_measurement_times]

Plain-English meaning  
Cut-off times decide when balances are measured. If Cash, Debt, WC, and Expenses use different times, parties can “win” via timing rather than economics.

Typical / Buyer-leaning / Seller-leaning patterns

- Typical: a single Reference Time applies across calculations; sometimes separate times for different closings/legs.
- Buyer-leaning: single cut-off time + explicit handling of deposits in transit/outstanding checks; allocate value drift for dual closings.
- Seller-leaning: more flexible timing or fewer explicit routing rules.

Pitfalls + drafting tells

- Different times for Cash vs Debt vs WC vs Expenses (mismatched numbers).
- “Closing Date” used with no time.
- Deposits in transit/outstanding checks not specified at the cut-off time.
- Separate Purchase Closing Time and Subscription Closing Time without a bridge.

Counsel questions

- What is the single cut-off time, and does it apply to all calculations and statements?
- How are deposits in transit and outstanding checks treated at that cut-off?
- If there are multiple closings/times, who bears value drift between them?

Likely pushback

- “Timing will wash out; it doesn’t matter.”

Rebuttals / fallback

- “It won’t wash out if cash moves between cut-offs; one sentence avoids timing disputes.”
- “If dual closing times are intentional, allocate the gap economics explicitly.”

Minimal edits

- “Reference Time” means 11:59 p.m. on the day immediately prior to Closing~~.~~ **All calculations of Cash, Indebtedness, Net Working Capital and Transaction Expenses shall be made as of the Reference Time.**
- Cash shall be calculated as of the Reference Time ~~without regard to deposits in transit or outstanding checks~~ **including deposits in transit and excluding outstanding checks**.
- Any cash movements between the Purchase Closing Time and Subscription Closing Time shall be ~~for Buyer’s account~~ **for Seller’s account** (or other stated allocation).
