### Definition: Purchase Price and Consideration
**Section Reference:** Article I defined terms “Purchase Price”, “Closing Transaction Consideration”, “Transaction Consideration”, PDF p. 17/109; p. 5/109; p. 21/109

**Full Verbatim Text:**

```text
“Purchase Price” means $175,000,000.

“Closing Transaction Consideration” means an amount equal to the sum of: (a) the Purchase Price; plus (b) the
Estimated Closing Working Capital Adjustment; plus (c) the Estimated Cash; minus (d) the Estimated Indebtedness; and
minus (e) the Estimated Transaction Expenses.

“Transaction Consideration” means the Closing Transaction Consideration, together with those portions of the
Escrow Funds and the Post-Closing Adjustment (if any) that an Equityholder actually receives pursuant to the terms of this
Agreement and the Escrow Agreement.
```

**Plain-English Summary:**

This SPA is priced on a cash-free/debt-free concept: the base $175m purchase price is adjusted at closing for estimated Cash, Indebtedness, Working Capital versus a target, and Transaction Expenses.
Equityholders ultimately receive ‘Transaction Consideration’ consisting of the closing cash paid to them plus subsequent escrow releases and any post-closing adjustment amounts.

```text
“Indebtedness” means, without duplication and with respect to the Company and its Subsidiaries, all: (a)
```

## Part 24: Executive Summary

### 24.1 Deal Overview

Brady Worldwide, Inc. (through BW Acquisition Corp.) acquires 100% of The Code Corporation from its stockholders for a $175.0m base purchase price, adjusted at closing for Cash, Indebtedness, Working Capital versus a $4.520m target (with a symmetric collar), and Transaction Expenses, with a post-closing true-up under Section 2.05.

Consideration is all-cash, with optionholders cashed out and payments administered via a paying agent based on a pre-close Consideration Spreadsheet.

### 24.2 Overall Assessment

|Dimension|Rating|Key drivers|
|---|---|---|
|Definitions clarity|🟠|Core terms exist and are usable, but several important edge cases are not explicit (restricted cash, overdrafts, deferred revenue).|
|Anti-duplication robustness|🟢|Strong routing between WC, Transaction Expenses and Indebtedness; indemnity anti-duplication clause backs this up.|
|Price mechanics clarity|🟠|Formula is clear but relies on Exhibit B and detailed schedules not provided; WC collar adds complexity.|
|True-up process fairness|🟢|90/30/60 timeline with IA limited to disputed items and range limitation; access rights support balance.|
|Escrow terms|🟠|Escrow is ~5.7% and survival is 6 months; seller-friendly compared to many no-RWI deals.|
|Overall balance Buyer ↔ Seller|🟠|Completion accounts structure is balanced, but narrow debt definition, WC collar, and short survival favor sellers slightly.|

### 24.3 Top 5 Issues to Raise with Partner or Client

1. **Exhibit B and methodology lock-down:** Obtain Sample Working Capital Calculation and align accounting hierarchy; without it, the true-up is dispute-prone.
2. **Debt definition gaps:** Confirm overdrafts, intercompany, lease liabilities, and L/C obligations; expand definition if any exist.
3. **Working Capital scope:** Confirm whether deferred revenue/customer deposits exist; add to WC or confirm immaterial.
4. **Cash edge cases:** Clarify restricted/trapped cash and overdraft netting; obtain bank rec support at Effective Time.
5. **Survival and cap adequacy:** Six-month survival and escrow cap may be light if diligence finds longer-tail risks; consider extensions or special indemnities.

### 24.4 30-Second Deal Summary

"Headline is $175.0m on a cash-free/debt-free style basis with completion accounts.

Key definitions: Indebtedness is **moderately narrow**—captures funded debt, capital leases, drawn L/C reimbursements and breakage, but is silent on overdrafts/intercompany/undrawn L/Cs. Cash includes equivalents and nets outstanding checks; it does not explicitly address restricted cash.

NWC: Target is $4.520m with a **collar**—no adjustment if NWC lands between $4.294m and $4.746m.

True-up: Buyer prepares final statement within 90 days; seller has 30 days to review and disputes go to an independent accountant within the parties’ ranges.

Escrows: $10.0m indemnity escrow (~5.7%) releasing at 6 months, plus a $275k stockholder rep expense fund.

Key risks: missing Exhibit B methodology and the gaps in Cash and Indebtedness edge-case treatment.

Overall: slightly seller-favorable due to the collar, narrower debt definition, and short survival."
