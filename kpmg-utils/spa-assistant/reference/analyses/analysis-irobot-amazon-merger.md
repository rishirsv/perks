> (a) Merger Consideration. Each Share issued and outstanding immediately prior to the Effective Time (other than (i) Shares owned by Parent

Part 22: Suggested Revisions

Revision 1: Tighten Expense Reimbursement scope and evidence requirements

Current Language (excerpt):

> “… pay to Parent… all of the reasonable and documented out-of-pocket expenses, including those of the Paying Agent… in an amount not to exceed $17,000,000 (the “Expense Reimbursement”)…” (Section 8.2(b), **A‑56 / PDF p. 59**)

Issue: “Reasonable and documented out-of-pocket expenses” can still create category disputes (internal allocations, duplicative advisor costs, financing diligence, etc.) and may be viewed as buyer-leaning given it applies on vote failure/outside date scenarios.

Suggested Revision (example):

- Define reimbursable categories (external legal, external financial advisor, regulatory filing fees, paying agent) and expressly exclude internal management time, overhead allocations, and costs incurred after termination notice.
- Require itemized invoices and certify no duplication with any termination fee amounts.

Rationale: Reduces disputes and prevents “double recovery” arguments.

Likely Pushback: Buyer may argue it needs flexibility for unpredictable regulatory and advisory expenses.

Fallback Position: Keep broad language but add an explicit exclusion for internal costs and a requirement for reasonable detail/invoice support.

Revision 2: Clarify interest concept for delayed stockholder payments caused by buyer breach

Current Language:

> “without interest” (Section 4.1(a), **A‑2–A‑3 / PDF pp. 5–6**) and “No interest will be paid or accrued…” (Section 4.2(c)(iii), **A‑4 / PDF p. 7**)

Issue: Standard, but if payment delays are driven by buyer breach or avoidable administrative issues, stockholders bear time value loss.

Suggested Revision:

- No interest generally, but interest accrues after X days if payment delay results from Parent’s willful breach or failure to fund Payment Fund.

Rationale: Aligns incentives and protects against extreme tail scenarios.

Likely Pushback: Buyer will resist any interest exposure.

Fallback Position: Limit interest only to court-adjudicated willful breach delays.

Revision 3: Confirm minimum D&O tail coverage if 300% cap binds

Current Language:

> “…provided, that in no event shall the annual cost… exceed… 300%… provided, further… obtain a policy with the greatest coverage available for a cost not exceeding such amount.” (Section 6.11(c), **A‑51–A‑52 / PDF pp. 54–55**)

Issue: “Greatest coverage available” is subjective; if market pricing spikes, insureds may end up with materially reduced terms.

Suggested Revision:

- Add objective minimums: Side A only, same limits/retentions if commercially available within cap; define “comparable” standards more tightly.

Rationale: Reduces ambiguity and protects directors/officers.

Likely Pushback: Buyer wants flexibility to manage cost.

Fallback Position: Add a requirement to consult with a representative of the pre-close Company Board or a D&O Indemnitee representative before finalizing a reduced-coverage policy.
