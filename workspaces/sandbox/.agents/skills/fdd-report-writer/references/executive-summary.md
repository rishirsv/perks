## Section objective

The Executive summary section brings the most important findings from the report to the forefront. In most cases, it synthesizes the current deal view from the full report contents provided, or from the specific workstream inputs the user supplies, in a format that is concise, quantified, and easy to scan.

It should present the most material findings across quality of earnings, net working capital, net debt, and any other report areas that materially affect valuation, closing mechanics, or confidence in the current view. It is a synthesis section, not a restatement of the full underlying analyses.

Global writing, placeholder, and language rules are defined in `references/global-writing-conventions.md` and apply here.

## Core principles

1. **Start from the deal view, not the process:** Surface the findings that matter most for valuation, closing, or confidence in the numbers rather than restating how the work was performed.
2. **Use the major workstreams as the default structure:** In most cases, organize the summary around quality of earnings, net working capital, net debt, and only the other areas that are genuinely material.
3. **Build the summary from the full evidence set:** Assess all provided report contents before deciding what belongs in the executive summary.
4. **Prioritize significance within each workstream:** Select findings based on deal relevance, absolute value, closing impact, classification impact, or analytical risk.
5. **Keep the sentence pattern tight and quantified:** Lead with a clear label, anchor the finding to an amount, unit, and period where possible, and then explain why it matters.
6. **Let non-core themes remain dynamic:** Include reporting environment, budget achievability, standalone costs, reconciliation issues, cash flow considerations, or other themes only when they materially affect the current view.
7. **Keep the section concise:** Pull forward the findings the reader needs to know now, not the full supporting analysis.

## Analytical workflow

1. **Identify the drafting request and default scope:** Confirm whether the user wants a full executive summary, a key findings section, or a key finding on a specific topic. If the user has not narrowed the request, treat it as a full executive summary.
2. **Inventory the available inputs:** Use the entire report when provided. Otherwise, use the specific sections, adjustment schedules, or findings the user supplied.
3. **Assess all provided content before drafting:** Scan the full evidence set to identify the most material findings rather than drafting from one workstream in isolation.
4. **Determine the workstreams or themes to include:** Default to quality of earnings, net working capital, and net debt when those analyses are available, then add only the other themes that are material enough to belong in the executive summary.
5. **Rank findings within each workstream:** Prioritize items by amount, risk, classification impact, closing impact, or usefulness to valuation.
6. **Group findings into the clearest structure:** For a full executive summary, use the major workstreams as the primary structure. Within adjustment-driven workstreams, use categories such as management adjustments, diligence adjustments, pro forma adjustments, or other considerations only when those categories improve clarity.
7. **Select only the strongest items:** As a rule of thumb, pull the 2-3 most significant items within each relevant category or workstream unless the source clearly supports a shorter or longer list.
8. **Draft in final reading order:** For a full executive summary, start with transaction context, then walk quality of earnings, net working capital, net debt, and only then any other material themes. For topic-specific requests, start with the requested topic and synthesize the most relevant evidence across the provided materials.
9. **Use the standard executive-summary pattern consistently:** Use a short context line where relevant, then a workstream or topic label, an optional one-line summary, and concise quantified finding bullets.
10. **Stop when the current deal view is clear:** Include enough detail for the reader to understand the major findings, the most material supporting items, and any remaining sensitivities without turning the section into a compressed restatement of the full report.

## Section architecture

Scale the section based on the number of material workstreams, the significance of the findings, and whether the user is asking for a full executive summary or a topic-specific key finding.

**Verbosity:**

- Stop adding detail when the reader can understand the key findings across the material workstreams, the most significant supporting items within those workstreams, and any major sensitivities that still affect the current view. In most cases, aim for 1-2 opening bullets plus 3-5 key finding groupings. Move above that range only when the report genuinely contains several major workstreams or the user asks for broader coverage.

**Required content areas:**

- Transaction context and scope
  - State what transaction is being considered and, where relevant, define the perimeter or period covered.
- Key findings by major workstream
  - Present the most important findings across the material workstreams, usually starting with quality of earnings, net working capital, and net debt when those analyses are in scope.

**Optional content areas:**

- Workstream categories
  - Add when adjustment-driven findings are easier to read when separated into categories such as diligence adjustments or pro forma adjustments.
- Other material themes
  - Add only when reporting environment, budget achievability, standalone costs, reconciliation issues, other cash flow considerations, or another report area materially affect the current view.
- Topic-specific key finding
  - Use when the user asks for a key finding on a specific topic rather than a full executive summary.
- Reporting basis note
  - Add when audit status, reconciliation status, or basis of preparation materially affects how the findings should be read.

## Data and drafting inputs

**Data / information typically needed:**

- The report sections completed so far, or the specific workstream materials the user has provided
- Transaction context and perimeter details when a full executive summary is being drafted
- Quality of earnings adjustments, including adjustment category, amount, and rationale
- Net working capital adjustments, including amount, rationale, and any related days metrics
- Net debt and debt-like items, including key balances, facilities, restrictions, or closing implications
- Any other significant findings from the report, such as reconciliation issues, standalone costs, reporting environment findings, or budget achievability observations
- The relevant period basis, such as FY24, TTM Jun-24, or closing-date basis

**Data mapping considerations:**

- Start with the full report contents when they are available. Do not draft the executive summary from one workstream alone if broader report content has been provided.
- Default to quality of earnings, net working capital, and net debt as the core executive-summary workstreams when those analyses are available.
- For quality of earnings, usually pull the 2-3 most significant items within each relevant category, ordered by significance or by the category structure used in the underlying analysis.
- For net working capital, usually pull the highest-value or highest-risk adjustments, especially where they affect operational liquidity, true-up mechanics, or days metrics.
- For net debt, focus on the largest debt and debt-like items, financing structure, and any restrictions or closing implications.
- Use other material themes dynamically. Pull in findings from any section of the report only when they are significant enough to belong in the executive summary.
- If the user asks for a key finding on a specific topic, scan all provided materials for that theme and write the finding in the same concise, quantified structure used for the major workstreams.
- If the user has already provided candidate key findings, refine, reorder, and tighten them rather than recreating the analysis from scratch.
- If source information is incomplete but the point still matters, use precise placeholders in square brackets rather than vague filler.

**Formatting principle:**

- Default to a short transaction-context bullet followed by labeled workstream or topic groupings.
- Within each grouping, use an optional one-line summary where helpful, followed by concise quantified finding bullets.
- Use simple labels such as `Quality of earnings`, `Net working capital`, `Net debt`, or `Other key findings`.
- For quantified findings, place the amount and unit in parentheses immediately after the label when that is the clearest format. Example: `Customer attrition (-$1.2 million Adjusted EBITDA):`

## Typical content areas

Use these as the main building blocks for the section. Choose only the content areas the draft actually needs.

The standard pattern is a short transaction context, followed by workstream or topic labels, an optional one-line summary where helpful, and concise quantified finding bullets.

### Transaction context

- Use when: drafting a full executive summary.
- Skip when: the user asks only for a topic-specific key finding.
- Keep this short and factual.
- Sample wording: `Orion Capital is considering a majority investment in HarborPay Solutions, with the analysis covering FY22-FY24 and TTM Jun-24.`

### Workstream or topic label

- Use a simple label to introduce each major grouping.
- Keep labels plain and source-consistent.
- Sample wording: `Quality of earnings`

### Workstream summary line

- Add a short top-line conclusion when the workstream has a headline metric or outcome that should be stated first.
- Skip this when the detailed findings alone are clear enough.
- Sample wording: `TTM Jun-24 pro forma EBITDA is $27.8 million after diligence and pro forma adjustments.`

### Key finding bullet

- This is the core executive-summary sentence pattern.
- State one quantified finding, anchor it to the relevant period or basis, and explain why it matters.
- Sample wording: `- Revenue-share reserve true-up (-$1.8 million Adjusted EBITDA): Reported results were revised using actual settlements through Jun-24 to remove period-to-period volatility from the reserve accrual.`

### Workstream category lead-in

- Use when: an adjustment-driven workstream is easier to scan when grouped into categories such as diligence adjustments or pro forma adjustments.
- Skip when: the workstream is already clear as a compact list of finding bullets.
- Sample wording: `Select diligence adjustments to TTM Jun-24 include the following:`

### Other material theme

- Use when: budget achievability, reporting environment, reconciliation issues, standalone costs, other cash flow considerations, or another report area materially affects the current view.
- Skip when: the point is secondary detail that belongs in the underlying section only.
- Sample wording: `- Reporting environment: Audited financial statements are available for FY22-FY24; however, TTM Jun-24 relies on management schedules reconciled to the trial balance, and selected balance-sheet mappings remain subject to final confirmation.`

### Topic-specific key finding

- Use when: the user asks for a key finding on reconciliation, reporting issues, standalone costs, or another specific theme.
- Skip when: drafting a standard full executive summary with the usual workstream structure.
- Sample wording: `Key finding on reconciliation issues: Historical working-capital and net debt analyses rely on a mix of audited FY24 balances and management schedules for TTM Jun-24. The most material reconciliation issues relate to intercompany clearing balances and payroll accrual mappings that remain subject to final confirmation across the in-scope entities.`

## Section-specific writing guidance

1. Use the same key-finding sentence pattern consistently throughout the section.
2. When a workstream is adjustment-driven, organize the findings by category before listing the most significant items only when that grouping improves readability.
3. Keep `Other key findings` flexible and driven by significance, not by a fixed checklist.
4. Avoid letting one workstream dominate the section unless the underlying report clearly supports that weighting.
5. Keep the tone decisive and evidence-based, but do not overstate unresolved findings.

## Verification checks

**Verification questions:**

- Does the section pull the most material findings from the full report contents or the specific materials the user provided?
- Where relevant, does the section use the standard major workstreams of quality of earnings, net working capital, net debt, and only the other themes that are genuinely material?
- Within adjustment-driven workstreams, are the findings grouped into sensible categories and limited to the most significant items?
- Are the findings quantified, period-anchored, and written in a consistent executive-summary sentence structure?
- If an additional report area is included, is it there because it is genuinely material to the current view?
- If the user asked for a key finding on a specific topic, does the draft clearly synthesize that topic from the relevant evidence provided?

## Full examples

These examples show content flow, not required headings or exact bullet counts.

The examples below are anonymized and illustrate the style and structure of the expected output.

### Example 1: Standard executive summary with major workstreams

```markdown
## Executive summary

- Orion Capital is considering a majority investment in HarborPay Solutions. The analysis covers FY22-FY24 and TTM Jun-24.

Quality of earnings
TTM Jun-24 pro forma EBITDA is $27.8 million after diligence and pro forma adjustments.
Select diligence adjustments to TTM Jun-24 include the following:
- Revenue-share reserve true-up (-$1.8 million Adjusted EBITDA): Reported results were revised using actual settlements through Jun-24 to remove period-to-period volatility from the reserve accrual.
- Settlement interest income (+$900,000 Adjusted EBITDA): Interest income earned on overnight settlement cash balances is retained as part of normal business operations.
Select pro forma adjustments to TTM Jun-24 relate to the following:
- New merchant wins (+$700,000 Adjusted EBITDA): Reflects the run-rate contribution of merchants onboarded during FY24 and H1 FY25, net of merchants lost during the same period.
- Pricing amendment (+$600,000 Adjusted EBITDA): Represents the impact of revised pricing under a processing agreement effective April 2024, applied across the historical period for comparability.

Net working capital
Select diligence adjustments to TTM Jun-24 include the following:
- Deferred revenue normalization (+$1.2 million): Removes balances relating to multi-period implementation services that do not reflect the day-to-day liquidity profile of the business.
- Bonus accrual reclassification (-$500,000): Includes annual management bonus accruals in working capital because the related expense is reflected in EBITDA and the liability recurs in the ordinary course.
Select pro forma adjustments to TTM Jun-24 relate to the following:
- Merchant receivables DSO normalization (-$1.4 million): Normalizes elevated DSO in the most recent period to 44 days, in line with the historical profile prior to a temporary billing dispute.

Net debt
Select debt and debt-like items as at Jun-24 include the following:
- Senior term facility ($41.8 million): Represents the primary funded debt instrument of the business and remains the largest net debt item at Jun-24.
- Transaction bonuses ($1.1 million): Reflects accrued change-of-control payments expected to be settled at closing.
- Lease liabilities ($4.6 million): Relate primarily to office and operations leases and are treated as debt-like on the current basis.

Other key findings
- Reporting environment: Audited financial statements are available for FY22-FY24; however, TTM Jun-24 relies on management schedules reconciled to the trial balance, and selected balance-sheet mappings remain subject to final confirmation.
- Budget achievability: Based on YTD Jun-24 trading and current merchant onboarding trends, the FY24 budget appears broadly achievable on an adjusted EBITDA basis.
```

### Example 2: Executive summary with additional material themes

```markdown
## Executive summary

- Alder Peak Partners is considering an acquisition of Meridian Health Services. The analysis covers FY23-FY24 and TTM Aug-24.

Quality of earnings
TTM Aug-24 pro forma EBITDA is $42.6 million after management, diligence, and pro forma adjustments.
Select diligence adjustments to TTM Aug-24 include the following:
- Provider payroll true-up (-$1.3 million Adjusted EBITDA): Reflects correction of payroll costs understated in the historical close process for certain clinics acquired during FY24.
- Non-recurring integration costs (+$800,000 Adjusted EBITDA): Removes one-time integration and systems migration costs relating to recently acquired practices.
Select pro forma adjustments to TTM Aug-24 relate to the following:
- Clinic maturation (+$1.1 million Adjusted EBITDA): Reflects the annualized earnings impact of locations opened during FY24 as they move toward mature run-rate levels.

Net working capital
Select diligence adjustments to TTM Aug-24 include the following:
- Insurance receivable reserve (-$2.1 million): Removes disputed receivables aged beyond 180 days from the normalized working-capital position.
- Acquisition-related accruals (+$900,000): Remove balances associated with pre-close acquisition and integration activities that are not part of ordinary-course working capital.

Net debt
Select debt and debt-like items as at Aug-24 include the following:
- Revolver borrowings ($18.4 million): Represent the primary short-term funding balance at Aug-24.
- Deferred consideration ($3.2 million): Reflects unpaid acquisition consideration associated with two FY24 tuck-in acquisitions.

Other key findings
- Other cash flow considerations: Valuation should reflect the timing of cash collections on aged insurance receivables and the near-term cash outflow associated with deferred acquisition consideration.
- Budget achievability: The FY24 budget appears achievable on a reported EBITDA basis; however, delivery of the pro forma case depends on clinic maturation and provider recruitment assumptions in Q4.
```

### Example 3: Topic-specific key finding request

```markdown
Key finding on reconciliation issues:
Historical working-capital and net debt analyses rely on a mix of audited FY24 balances and management schedules for TTM Jun-24. The most material reconciliation issues relate to intercompany clearing balances and payroll accrual mappings that remain subject to final confirmation across the in-scope entities. These items do not currently change the direction of the deal view, but they may affect final classification between net debt, working capital, and other balance-sheet captions.
```
