### Business overview (`business_overview`)

#### Common

- (scope.001) Meet with Target's officers and management in order to develop an understanding of operations, including its:
  - Basis of financial information;
  - Organization structure;
  - Historical growth by geography; and
  - Finance function, financial reporting framework and internal control environment.

#### Industry (`manufacturing`)

- (scope.221) Obtain background information of Target, including its:
  - Organizational structure, ownership, and management reporting relationships;
  - Inter-group operations and allocation of corporate overheads, where applicable;
  - Range of products and services, degree of customization, customer segments, and regions served;
  - Production lines capacity, utilization, scrap rates, and key operating metrics;
  - Pricing methodology and key contractual terms with customers, including ability to pass through input cost increases;
  - Key supplier dependencies, contractual terms, and raw material price volatility;
  - Marketing and distribution channels, where applicable;
  - Research and new product development initiatives, where applicable; and
  - Financial reporting and information systems (including process, timing, structure of financial reporting, and interaction with the information/accounting systems).

### Accounting overview (`accounting_overview`)

#### Common

- (scope.002) Read Target's financial statements and discuss Target's accounting policies and practices with management, including:
  - Finance function, financial reporting framework, and management reporting relationships (including processes, timing, structure of financial reporting, and interaction with the information/accounting systems);
  - Significant accounting policies and recent or contemplated changes in accounting principles, procedures, or estimates;
  - Significant accounting estimates (e.g. bad debt provision, rebates, capitalized R&D, etc.); and
  - Intercompany accounts and related party transactions, if any.
- (scope.004) Consider if the following accounting policies have been applied consistently within and across historical periods:
  - Cash versus accrual accounting policies;
  - Capitalization policies;
  - Depreciation policies; and
  - Differences between interim and year-end procedures; and
  - Revenue recognition policies;

#### Industry (`manufacturing`)

- (scope.224) Read Target's financial statements and discuss them with Target's management to gain an understanding of accounting policies and practices including:
  - Reporting methodology;
  - Revenue recognition policies;
  - Frequency of standard cost updates and procedures for analyzing variances;
  - Significant accounting estimates (e.g. bad debt provision, rebates, capitalized R&D, etc.); and
  - Recent or contemplated changes in accounting principles.

### Quality of earnings (`quality_of_earnings`)

#### Common

- (scope.005) Identify, and where possible quantify, potential earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization ("EBITDA") normalization items by considering:
  - The impact of the normalization adjustments identified by Management;
  - Large, unusual or non-recurring events, or transactions that may have distorted results;
  - The impact of any changes to senior management or management team structure;
  - The impact of provisions (allowance for doubtful accounts, inventory obsolescence, other), management estimates or adjusting entries on the reported results;
  - The impact of foreign currency transactions;
  - The impact of related party transactions and shared services provided by other entities, if applicable;
  - The impact of any changes in accounting policies, procedures and estimates; and
  - Other potential items identified during the due diligence process.

#### Industry (`manufacturing`)

- (scope.230) Identify potential adjustments to reported earnings before interest, income taxes, depreciation, and amortization ("EBITDA") by considering the impact of:
  - Management's proposed adjustments to reported EBITDA (e.g. out-of period inventory adjustments, non-recurring professional fees, Dot customer normalizations etc.);
  - The impact of acquisitions (e.g. acquired businesses);
  - The impact of supply chain disruptions, if any;
  - The impact of research and development expenditures and capitalization policies;
  - The timing of true-ups and other adjustments made less frequently than monthly;
  - The impact of any changes to senior management or team structure;
  - The impacts of foreign exchange including constant currency analysis;
  - The impact of commodity / raw material cost fluctuations and any associated hedging programs;
  - The impact of any key supplier cost arrangements including assessment of hedged vs. market prices and illustrative impacts to EBITDA;
  - The impact of lease renewal;
  - The impact of related party transactions (i.e. non-fair market pricing, personal expenses, donations etc.); and
  - Other potential items discovered in the due diligence process.

### Revenue analysis (`revenue_analysis`)

#### Common

- (scope.006) Obtain a comprehensive view of revenue and margins. Obtain underlying transactional detail, if available, and gain an understanding of major trends in revenue and margins, including:
  - Revenue and margin by significant customer, channel, and/or market segment, as applicable; and
  - Price, volume, and mix trends.
- (scope.007) Discuss with management key drivers of revenue and margin over the Historical Period, including:
  - Major customer/contract wins or losses and timing impacts;
  - Non-recurring items affecting period results; and
  - Operational, market, or seasonal factors affecting demand and fulfillment.
- (scope.008) Obtain and review an analysis of revenue quality and related balances, and inquire about:
  - Revenue recognition policies and any recent changes;
  - Billing and collection terms, including significant deviations from standard terms;
  - Credits, rebates, returns, and other contra-revenue items;
  - Concentration/dependency risk; and
  - Other unusual items affecting reported revenue or gross margin.

#### Industry (`manufacturing`)

- (scope.233) Obtain a comprehensive view of revenue, value add, and contribution margin (pending data availability) by product line and customer. Obtain underlying transactional data (manufacturing orders database), if available, and analyze major trends in revenue and margins, including:
  - Gross-to-net revenue trends;
  - Revenue and gross margin by plant, brand, product category, customer, and SKU;
  - Price, volume, and mix trends, including organic growth considerations;
  - Revenue seasonality and any non-recurring revenue components;
  - Raw material costs, hedging programs, and embedded inventory gains/losses;
  - Breakdown of cost of sales, including raw materials, scrap/waste, labour, manufacturing overhead, and freight; and
  - Ability to pass through changes in raw material and other input costs.

### Operating expenses (`operating_expenses`)

#### Common

- (scope.009) Obtain and read an analysis of Target’s expenses and inquire about:
  - Costing methodology;
  - Cost of sale trends, impact of material change in vendors (if any);
  - Employee compensation and related costs by function;
  - Selling, general, and administrative expenses;
  - Repairs and maintenance expense;
  - Unusual and extraordinary items (if any).

#### Industry (`manufacturing`)

- (scope.236) Obtain and read an analysis of Target's operating expenses and inquire about:
  - High-level overview of employee compensation and benefits including:
    - Headcount and average compensation costs;
    - Accrued employee obligations and other provisions;
    - Severance / commitments, if any;
    - Incentive based compensation;
    - Flag any material gaps in headcount; and
    - Reconcile employee census to the financial statements.
  - Occupancy, rent, and logistics costs;
  - Food safety and quality costs;
  - Research and development costs;
  - Selling, advertising, and marketing expenses;
  - Professional fees and other general and administrative expenses;
  - Fixed vs. variable expense analysis; and
  - Unusual and extraordinary items.

### Working capital (`working_capital`)

#### Common

- (scope.010) Obtain monthly details of the Company's consolidated working capital and analyze and comment on:
  - The composition of individual working capital accounts (e.g., trade receivables, accounts payable and accruals, etc.);
  - Monthly working capital trends, metrics, and seasonality;
  - Large, unusual, or non-operating items that may have affected normal working capital trends (such as accruals for bonuses, capital accruals, month end vs quarter end differences, etc.); and
  - Understanding the accounting impact of allowance for uncollectible amounts and other accruals requiring judgment.

#### Industry (`manufacturing`)

- (scope.249) Summarize and normalize historical working capital in the business, including:
  - Historical working capital trends for the respective periods;
  - Key drivers and trade-ratio trends (e.g., seasonality, DSO, DIO, and DPO);
  - Large or unusual non-recurring items that may have impacted normal working capital trends, including items identified within the quality of earnings analysis;
  - Historical impact from acquisitions and associated normalizations;
  - Payroll-related funding and timing of cash flows; and
  - Approach to determine a normalized net working capital target/peg.
