#### Purchase Price Components Taxonomy

Below is a practical “taxonomy” I use to decompose _any_ SPA purchase price into clean building blocks (and then rebuild it into the seller’s net proceeds and the buyer’s total cost). 📌

---

### A) Headline Price / Base Consideration (the “big number”)

1. **Headline Purchase Price / Base Purchase Price**
   - The stated starting point (may be called “Purchase Price,” “Base Purchase Price,” “Total Consideration,” etc.).
   - Can be expressed as:
     - **Enterprise Value (EV)** (“cash-free, debt-free” pricing basis), or
     - **Equity Value** (already incorporates cash/debt), or
     - **Per-share price** (public deals / equity rollups).

2. **Form(s) of Consideration (what the seller actually receives)**
   - **Cash at Closing**
   - **Buyer Equity / Stock Consideration** (public shares, private shares, units, exchange ratio)
   - **Rollover Equity / Reinvestment** (seller retains/reinvests into NewCo)
   - **Seller Note / Promissory Note** (deferred, interest-bearing)
   - **Assumption of Liabilities / Debt** (buyer assumes/doesn’t require payoff)
   - **Non-cash property** (rare: assets, IP, etc.)

---

### B) Closing Adjustments (Completion Accounts / Closing Accounts mechanics)

These are the “bridge” items that convert the headline into **Closing Equity Value**.

3. **Net Working Capital (NWC) / Net Current Assets Adjustment**
   - Adjusts price up/down based on actual NWC delivered at close versus a negotiated **target/peg**.

4. **Cash and Cash Equivalents Adjustment**
   - If priced cash-free: buyer typically pays extra for cash left in the business (or cash is swept pre-close).

5. **Indebtedness / Net Debt Adjustment**
   - If priced debt-free: buyer reduces price for debt-like items, or requires payoff at close.

6. **Transaction Expenses Adjustment**
   - Often reduces purchase price for seller-borne deal costs (banker fees, legal, bonuses) that exist at closing.

7. **Leakage / Permitted Leakage (Locked-Box structures)**
   - Instead of closing accounts: price is fixed as of a “locked-box date,” then adjusted for leakage and sometimes a ticking fee.

8. **Other “bespoke” adjustments**
   - Inventory adjustments, A/R collectability mechanics, deferred revenue, specific reserves, intercompany balances, capex true-ups, tax-related balance sheet items, etc.
   - Carve-out deals sometimes use a **Net Assets / Net Working Capital** variant.

---

### C) Withheld Amounts (security / mechanics that affect cash at close)

These don’t change the _agreed_ price; they change **timing** and **certainty** of proceeds.

9. **General Indemnity Escrow / Holdback**
   - Portion of price withheld to satisfy indemnity claims.

10. **Purchase Price Adjustment (PPA) Escrow**

- Separate escrow dedicated to resolving the post-close closing statement / NWC / net debt true-up.

11. **Special Purpose Escrows**

- Tax escrow, environmental escrow, litigation escrow, customer credit escrow, etc.

> Note: “Escrow” is third-party; “holdback” is buyer-held. Economically similar (cash withheld + release mechanics differ).

---

### D) Deferred & Contingent Consideration (changes total value, not just timing)

12. **Deferred Consideration (fixed, time-based)**

- Installments, delayed closing payments, deferred purchase price not contingent on performance.

13. **Earnout (performance-based contingent consideration)**

- Additional purchase price tied to EBITDA, revenue, ARR, product milestones, etc.

14. **Milestone / Contingent Payments (non-financial triggers)**

- FDA approval, customer contract win, patent grant, integration milestone.

15. **Price Ratchets / Collar Mechanisms (mostly public or structured deals)**

- Exchange ratio collars, price protection adjustments, measurement-period VWAP mechanics.

---

### E) “Proceeds-splitting” economics (not always called purchase price, but affects payout)

16. **Option/RSU/Warrant cash-out mechanics**
17. **Management carve-outs / transaction bonuses funded from proceeds**
18. **Withholding taxes (e.g., FATCA, payroll withholdings, non-resident withholding, etc.)**
19. **Set-off rights (buyer nets claims against future payments)**
20. **Interest mechanics** (interest on seller note; interest on escrows; ticking fees)

#### Key Terms per Component

Think of this as your SPA extraction checklist 🧾—the terms that actually drive economics.

---

### 1) Headline / Base Purchase Price

**Extract:**

- **Price basis**: EV vs Equity Value vs per-share
- **Currency** and FX mechanism (if cross-border)
- **“Cash-free, debt-free”** language (or not)
- **Any baked-in assumptions** (target NWC, net debt assumed = 0, etc.)
- **Defined term hierarchy**: which definition governs if inconsistent (agreement vs schedules vs example calc)

**Economic outcome drivers:**

- Whether the “headline” is **EV** (needs adjustments) or **Equity** (already adjusted)
- Any **fixed-price** vs **floating** features

---

### 2) Cash at Closing (payment mechanics)

**Extract:**

- Amount and timing (wire timing, same-day funds)
- Payees (seller rep, paying agent, escrow agent)
- **Deductions from the closing wire** (escrows, holdbacks, debt payoffs, expenses, withholdings)
- Funds flow exhibit / payment instructions mechanics

**Outcome drivers:**

- What comes off the top at closing vs paid later
- Whether buyer can **unilaterally deduct** disputed amounts

---

### 3) Stock / Equity Consideration (buyer shares)

**Extract:**

- Fixed shares vs fixed value
- Valuation methodology (VWAP period, reference price date, collar)
- Fractional shares treatment (cash in lieu?)
- Lock-up / leak-out restrictions
- Registration rights / liquidity rights
- Tax treatment language (if any)

**Outcome drivers:**

- How value is measured (signing vs closing vs average price window)
- Restrictions that make “paper value” not equal cash value

---

### 4) Rollover Equity / Reinvestment

**Extract:**

- % rollover or dollar amount
- Instrument: common vs preferred; liquidation prefs; distributions
- Governance: board, vetoes, drag/tag, information rights
- Transfer restrictions, call/put, vesting / forfeiture (if tied to employment)
- Valuation basis relative to headline price (discount? same multiple?)

**Outcome drivers:**

- Whether rollover is **real equity** or effectively **retention/incentive** with forfeiture hooks
- Exit rights and preference stack

---

### 5) Seller Note / Promissory Note (seller financing)

**Extract:**

- Principal, interest rate, compounding
- Maturity, amortization schedule, prepayment terms
- Security / guarantee (or expressly unsecured)
- Subordination / intercreditor provisions
- Covenants, events of default, acceleration
- **Set-off rights** (buyer can net indemnity/PPA claims against note)

**Outcome drivers:**

- Credit risk + subordination + set-off = real collectability

---

### 6) Net Working Capital (NWC) Adjustment

**Extract:**

- Definition of NWC (included/excluded accounts; “puts/takes”)
- **Target / peg** and how set
- Accounting standard: GAAP vs consistent with historical practices vs bespoke “Accounting Rules”
- Estimated closing statement vs final closing statement mechanics
- Timing: preparation, review period, dispute period, independent accountant
- Collar/bucket/cap/floor (if any)
- Payment mechanics: cash payment vs escrow release vs set-off

**Outcome drivers:**

- The **definition** (what counts) and **accounting rules** (how measured)
- Whether buyer can introduce “purchase accounting” (should be excluded)
- Process asymmetry: buyer controls books post-close (so access & timelines matter)

> Timing benchmarks often seen: buyer prepares final closing statement around **60–90 days after close**, with a defined seller review/dispute window. ([PwC][1])

---

### 7) Cash Adjustment

**Extract:**

- Definition of “Cash” / “Cash Equivalents” (restricted cash? deposits? in-transit? cash in drawers?)
- Whether cash is included in NWC (avoid double counting)
- Measurement time (close of business vs funds received)
- Treatment of checks/ACH outstanding

**Outcome drivers:**

- Definition + cut-off timing + double-count prevention

---

### 8) Indebtedness / Net Debt Adjustment

**Extract:**

- Definition of “Indebtedness” (debt-like items, leases, accrued interest, change-of-control payments, unpaid taxes, intercompany)
- Whether “debt” includes working-capital-like accruals (often contentious)
- Payoff letter mechanics and leakage between measurement and payoff
- Netting rules (cash netting?)

**Outcome drivers:**

- Debt definition is where “surprise value leakage” hides

---

### 9) Transaction Expenses Adjustment

**Extract:**

- Definition of “Transaction Expenses” (banker fees, legal, QoE, bonuses, retention, insurance premium split)
- Whether expenses are **accrued** vs only **unpaid**
- Whether expenses reduce price dollar-for-dollar
- Payment mechanics at close (paid from proceeds vs separately)

**Outcome drivers:**

- If the definition is broad, it can materially reduce cash to sellers

---

### 10) Locked-Box / Leakage (if applicable)

**Extract:**

- Locked-box date and reference financials
- Permitted leakage categories + caps
- Prohibited leakage definition
- Interest / ticking fee: rate, day-count, compounding, from when to when
- Claims process and evidence standard

**Outcome drivers:**

- Leakage definitions and proof burden
- Whether buyer can treat ordinary-course items as leakage

---

### 11) Escrows / Holdbacks (general indemnity + PPA + special)

**Extract:**

- Amount (% or $), funded from what (cash/stock)
- Purpose (general indemnity vs PPA vs special)
- Release schedule (single release vs staged)
- Survival alignment (escrow release vs reps survival)
- Claims process: notice requirements, minimums, documentation
- Interest on escrow (who gets it)
- Exclusive remedy language (is escrow the only source?)

**Outcome drivers:**

- Size + duration + ability to claim = real expected value haircut

---

### 12) Earnout / Contingent Consideration

**Extract:**

- Metric definition (EBITDA? revenue? ARR?) + **exact calculation rules**
- Measurement periods and reporting cadence
- Thresholds, cap, floor, linear vs tiered payout
- Control provisions: covenants on operation, integration, budgeting, salesforce changes
- Information rights + audit rights
- Dispute resolution (accountant vs arbitrator vs court)
- Acceleration (change of control; termination without cause; divestiture)
- Set-off rights (can buyer net claims against earnout?)

**Outcome drivers:**

- Definitions + operational control + ability to “manage the metric”
- Set-off and dispute process: earnouts are frequently litigated for a reason

#### Purchase Price Equation Format

Here’s the **30-second partner-friendly** format that reliably lands. ✅
It’s a **bridge** from headline → closing equity value → cash at close → total potential consideration.

---

### Recommended structure (template)

**1) Headline / Pricing Convention**

- **Headline EV (cash-free, debt-free):** $\_\_\_
  _(or Headline Equity Value / Fixed Price / Locked-Box Price)_

**2) Bridge to Closing Equity Value**

- **– Indebtedness (as defined):** (\_\_\_)
- **+ Cash (as defined):** \_\_\_
- **+/– NWC true-up vs Peg:** \_\_\_
- **– Seller transaction expenses (if applicable):** (\_\_\_)
- **+/– Other adjustments (leakage, tax, bespoke):** **\_
  = **Closing Equity Value:** $\_**

**3) What seller actually receives at Closing**

- **Less: Indemnity escrow/holdback:** (\_\_\_) _(% and release timing)_
- **Less: PPA escrow (if separate):** (\_\_\_) _(% and release timing)_
- **Less: deferred / note funded at close:** (\_\_\_)
- **Plus: buyer shares issued (if any):** \_\_\_ _(valuation basis + lock-up)_
- **Plus: rollover equity (if any):** **\_ *(valuation + rights)*
  = **Cash to Sellers at Closing:** $\_**

**4) Post-closing upside / downside**

- **Earnout:** up to $\_\_\_ _(metric, period, cap, set-off?)_
- **Deferred payments / note:** $**_ due _** _(interest, security)_
- **Expected PPA range:** approx +/– $\_\_\_ _(key drivers: NWC definition, debt-like items)_

---

### The “30-second spoken summary” (script)

> “Headline is **$X EV** on a cash-free/debt-free basis with an **NWC peg of $Y**. At close, sellers receive **$A cash**, after **$B of escrows/holdbacks** and normal debt/expense true-ups. Post-close, there’s a **working capital / net debt true-up** (timed ~60–90 days), and sellers can earn up to **$C earnout** over **\_\_ months/years** based on **[metric]**, subject to **[cap/set-off/control terms]**.”

(If locked-box, swap in: “fixed price as of [date] with leakage protections + ticking fee.”)

#### Common Mistakes

These are the ones that burn time (and credibility) most often ⚠️:

1. **Mixing up EV and Equity Value**
   - Treating “$100m purchase price” as cash to equity holders when it’s actually EV subject to net debt + NWC.

2. **Assuming “cash-free, debt-free” is standard—and then not reading the definitions**
   - The whole deal lives in the defined terms for **Cash**, **Indebtedness**, **NWC**, **Transaction Expenses**.

3. **Double counting (or missing) items across NWC vs Cash vs Debt**
   - Classic: including cash in NWC while also having a separate cash adjustment.

4. **Missing “transaction expenses” as a _purchase price adjustment_**
   - Analysts often treat it as a funds flow-only item; the SPA may treat it as a _price reducer_.

5. **Treating a working capital peg as “normalized” without checking seasonality and one-offs**
   - Pegs often embed an advantage to one side.

6. **Ignoring the accounting rules / precedence**
   - GAAP vs historical practices vs bespoke schedule: the precedence order is everything.

7. **Overlooking collars/buckets/caps/floors on adjustments**
   - A “collar” can turn a seemingly symmetric adjustment into a de facto one-way mechanic.

8. **Not mapping _who prepares_ the closing statement and who controls books post-close**
   - Buyer control + tight seller timelines can materially affect outcomes.

9. **Confusing PPA escrow vs indemnity escrow**
   - Different purposes, different release triggers, different claim standards.

10. **Not noting set-off rights**

- If buyer can set-off against earnout/note, contingent value is less “real.”

11. **Assuming earnout metric = GAAP metric**

- Earnout definitions frequently diverge from GAAP / statutory reporting.

12. **Missing earnout acceleration / forfeiture triggers**

- Change-of-control, termination of seller-employee, divestiture, integration can change payout.

13. **Not distinguishing “exclusive remedy” vs “first source”**

- If escrow is exclusive remedy, buyer’s recovery is capped to escrow (big economic point).

14. **Not aligning escrow release with reps survival**

- If reps survive longer than escrow, buyer may still pursue sellers directly (unless limited).

15. **Forgetting taxes/withholdings and proceeds-splitting economics**

- Withholding and payout waterfall (options, prefs, bonuses) can shift “net to common” materially.

#### Market Benchmarks

These are **real-world ranges** you can cite as “market” (with the usual caveat: depends on size, auction dynamics, industry risk, and R&W insurance). 📊

---

### Escrows / Holdbacks (general indemnity)

- **Prevalence:** escrow funds are used in the large majority of private deals (SRS reports **91%** include escrow). ([SRS Acquiom][2])
- **Size (non-insured deals):**
  - “Typical” **~10%** is a common anchor; SRS reports median escrow size **10%** when no M&A insurance is used. ([SRS Acquiom][2])
  - Seyfarth (middle market survey): median indemnity escrow **~9%** for non-insured deals. ([Seyfarth Shaw - Homepage][3])
  - Broader “ordinary sale transactions” range cited by a law firm: **5%–20%**. ([Wiggin and Dana LLP][4])

- **Size (insured / RWI-heavy deals):**
  - SRS reports median escrow **0.5%** when reps & warranties insurance is used. ([SRS Acquiom][2])
  - Seyfarth reports median escrow **0.3%** in insured deals. ([Seyfarth Shaw - Homepage][3])

### Escrow / holdback duration & reps survival (high-level)

- **Indemnity escrow period:** commonly around **12 months** (Seyfarth median = 12 months). ([Seyfarth Shaw - Homepage][3])
- **General reps survival:** often around **18 months** in non-insured deals (Seyfarth median = 18 months), and **12 months** in insured deals. ([Seyfarth Shaw - Homepage][3])
- Practical framing: buyers often have **~12–18 months** post-close to identify issues tied to reps. ([SRS Acquiom][2])

---

### Purchase price adjustments (PPA) and working capital mechanics

- **How common are PPAs?**
  - SRS indicates PPAs are in **>90%** of deals. ([SRS Acquiom][5])
  - ABA-referenced summary: purchase price adjustments in **93%** of deals; working capital used in **87%** (2021 ABA study). ([JD Supra][6])

- **Adjustment escrow size (“PPA escrow”)**
  - Rule-of-thumb frequently cited: **~1% of deal value**. ([Goodwin Law Firm][7])
  - Goodwin database: median adjustment escrow **<1% on larger deals**; **>1% on smaller deals**, with some small deals as high as **14%**; deals **$100m+ usually <1% and rarely >2%**. ([Goodwin Law Firm][7])
  - SRS: median size of a separate PPA escrow is about **1%**. ([SRS Acquiom][5])

- **Typical magnitude of PPA outcomes**
  - SRS: average adjustment amount **owed to buyers ~0.9% of transaction value** (when owed). ([SRS Acquiom][5])

- **Timing benchmarks (closing statement / true-up)**
  - Final closing statement commonly prepared by buyer **~60–90 days after close**. ([PwC][1])
  - Example timeline commonly drafted: buyer notice within **90 days**, seller dispute window **~30 days**. ([SRS Acquiom][8])

- **Working capital collars**
  - Not yet “standard,” but used with some frequency: Goodwin reports collars in **28% of middle-market** and **17% of lower middle-market** deals (their PE deals database). ([JD Supra][9])
  - **Width benchmark:** there’s _less published, consistent data_ on collar _size_ (vs existence). In practice, collars are usually sized as an **immateriality band** (often a fixed $ amount tied to expected NWC volatility), rather than a universal % that travels well across deal sizes.

---

### Earnouts (quick “market” anchors)

- **Prevalence varies by dataset and cycle:**
  - ABA deal-point commentary: earnouts were **~26%** (2023 study period) and **~18%** (2025 study period). ([K&L Gates][10])
  - Seyfarth middle market (2024/2025): earnouts in **13%** of deals. ([Seyfarth Shaw - Homepage][3])

- **Earnout performance reality check:**
  - SRS notes earnouts pay about **$0.21 per $1.00** of potential consideration (i.e., meaningful fall-off vs headline “up to”). ([SRS Acquiom][5])
  - SRS also notes that earnouts often use multiple metrics and that earnout periods >4 years were not seen in their 2024 closed deals data. ([SRS Acquiom][5])

---

### If you want one “benchmark slide” takeaway (what partners usually care about)

- **Escrow:** ~9–10% non-insured; ~0.3–0.5% insured; **~12 months** typical period. ([Seyfarth Shaw - Homepage][3])
- **PPA escrow:** ~**1%** rule-of-thumb; typically smaller in $100m+ deals. ([Goodwin Law Firm][7])
- **True-up timing:** **60–90 days** post-close. ([PwC][1])
- **Earnouts:** prevalence in the ~teens to ~20s% depending on dataset; payout often materially below “up to.” ([Seyfarth Shaw - Homepage][3])
