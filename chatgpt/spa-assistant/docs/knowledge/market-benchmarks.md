# M&A Market Benchmarks

This document consolidates market standard benchmarks for SPA terms, sourced from industry studies and practitioner surveys. Use these benchmarks to assess whether specific deal terms are market-standard, buyer-favorable, or seller-favorable.

> **Caveat:** "Market standard" varies by deal size, auction dynamics, industry risk, geographic jurisdiction, and whether R&W insurance is used. These benchmarks represent typical ranges in North American private-company M&A.

## Definition Standards

### Indebtedness (Market Standard)

Includes:
- Borrowed money + accrued interest + breakage
- Capital/finance leases
- Prepayment premiums/make-whole amounts
- Unamortized debt issuance costs/OID

Excludes:
- Ordinary course operating liabilities (A/P, accruals)

Should have:
- Limited catch-all tethered to debt-like concepts
- Reference schedule of facilities

### Cash (Market Standard)

Includes:
- Cash and cash equivalents

Excludes:
- Restricted cash

Should specify:
- Clear approach to transit items
- Overdrafts typically treated as Indebtedness
- Measurement timestamp (Effective Time or close of business)

### Working Capital (Market Standard)

Includes:
- Operating current assets minus operating current liabilities

Excludes:
- Cash, Indebtedness, Transaction Expenses

Best practice:
- Sample working capital schedule (example calculation)
- Account-level inclusions/exclusions schedule

### Accounting Principles (Market Standard Hierarchy)

1. SPA definitions + example schedules
2. Consistent with company's historical Financial Statements
3. GAAP/IFRS (as tie-breaker)
