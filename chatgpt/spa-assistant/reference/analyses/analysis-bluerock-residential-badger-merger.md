Part 2: Financial Definitions Deep Extraction

Definition 1: Purchase Price / Consideration

Definition Name: “Merger Consideration”

Section Reference: Section 3.1(a)(iii), PDF p. 19–20 (A-16–A-17)

Full Verbatim Text:

```
(iii) Conversion of Shares of Company Common Stock. At the Effective Time, by virtue of the Merger and
without any action on the part of the holders thereof, each share of Company Common Stock issued and
outstanding immediately prior to the Effective Time (other than Cancelled Shares) shall be automatically
converted into the right to receive $24.25 in cash, without interest (the “Merger Consideration”), as may be
adjusted pursuant to Section 3.1(c) or Section 6.16.
```

Plain-English Summary:
Each outstanding share of Company common stock (excluding “Cancelled Shares”) is cashed out for $24.25 in cash at the Effective Time, with no interest. The only explicit economic adjustments referenced are an equitable adjustment for stock splits and similar events, and a reduction if the Company pays a defined “Special Pre-Closing Dividend.”

| Phrase                                         | Why It’s Concerning                                                                                                                                                                                                                       | Severity |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------- |
| “as may be adjusted pursuant to… Section 6.16” | Only Special Pre-Closing Dividend is expressly price-adjusting; regular dividends are handled separately, but stakeholders can still misread whether other dividend types reduce the $24.25. Ambiguity increases execution disputes risk. | Medium   |

Overall Market Position Assessment: 🟢 Market Standard

Reasoning: Fixed cash per share with conventional mechanical adjustments is market for merger agreements. There is no completion accounts framework, which is normal for this style; economic “balance sheet” risk is priced into the $24.25.

Counsel Questions to Raise:

1. Confirm explicitly (in closing checklist / disclosure) that only the “Special Pre-Closing Dividend” reduces the $24.25, and that permitted regular dividends are not intended to adjust consideration (to avoid later “double dip” arguments).
2. Confirm that “Cancelled Shares” includes treasury shares and shares held by Parent/Merger Sub (and clarify treatment of any DRS/book-entry oddities) to ensure the cash funding amount is correct.

Suggested Revisions: Slight clarity enhancement (not strictly off-market, but reduces disputes)
Original: “as may be adjusted pursuant to Section 3.1(c) or Section 6.16.”
Suggested: “as may be adjusted pursuant to Section 3.1(c) and reduced only as expressly provided in Section 6.16 (and not for any other dividends).”
Rationale: Removes interpretive friction around dividend economics.

Part 22: Suggested Revisions

Revision 1: Clarify dividend economics and Merger Consideration adjustments

Current Language:

- “...$24.25 in cash… (the ‘Merger Consideration’), as may be adjusted pursuant to Section 3.1(c) or Section 6.16.” (Section 3.1(a)(iii); PDF p. 19–20)

Issue: The Agreement adjusts price for a Special Pre-Closing Dividend but handles other dividends through payment timing, which can create interpretive disputes about whether other dividends should reduce the $24.25.

Suggested Revision:

- Add explicit statement that the Merger Consideration is reduced only by the Special Pre-Closing Dividend (and equity-structure events) and is not otherwise adjusted for permitted dividends.

Rationale: Removes a predictable execution dispute class and makes funding computations cleaner.

Likely Pushback: Buyer may argue this is already clear by structure and cross-references.

Fallback Position: Add a definition/clarification in the dividend section instead of modifying the core conversion clause.

Revision 2: Strengthen Company enforcement rights where financing is available

Current Language:

- Specific enforcement / limitation construct in Section 9.5 (PDF p. 73–74), which limits remedies and references limited guarantee/equity commitment mechanics.

Issue: If Parent fails to close despite having funds available, the Company may be limited to fee-based recovery rather than forcing closing, which can be seller-off-market depending on deal context.

Suggested Revision:

- Permit Company to seek specific performance to cause closing when (i) all conditions are satisfied, (ii) equity commitment is fundable, and (iii) debt financing (if any) is available.

Rationale: Aligns remedies with the “no financing condition” concept and reduces execution risk for sellers.

Likely Pushback: Sponsor will resist broad specific performance and prefer a capped reverse fee.

Fallback Position: Keep remedy cap but add expedited injunction and funding covenant enforceability plus increased reverse fee if willful breach.

Revision 3: Add separation dispute governance and deliverables schedule

Current Language:

- Annex A provides principles-level allocation and contemplates additional agreements. (Annex A; PDF p. 80–87)

Issue: Principles without a dispute mechanism and account-level deliverables can create last-minute execution risk and post-close friction.

Suggested Revision:

- Add a schedule of deliverables (asset/liability schedules, intercompany settlement statements, tax allocation schedules) and a dispute resolution path (executive escalation then neutral accountant/arbitrator limited to allocation issues).

Rationale: Converts “principles” into operational certainty.

Likely Pushback: Parties may claim these are already being handled in implementation agreements.

Fallback Position: Require that the implementation agreements be executed by a specified date and be in form reasonably satisfactory to both parties, with key terms summarized in an exhibit.
