Definition Name: “Aggregate Purchase Price”

Section Reference: Section 2.1, Page 10

Full Verbatim Text:

```text
2.1 Aggregate Purchase Price. For purposes of this Agreement, the term “Aggregate Purchase Price” means an amount equal to the sum of
(a) $17,550,000 (the “Initial Purchase Price”), plus (b) the aggregate Cash balances of the Company and Elmwood immediately prior to the Closing
Time (the “Closing Cash”), plus (c) $1,950,000 (the “Escrow Amount”) to be paid to Wells Fargo Bank, N.A. to be held in escrow pursuant to the
```

Plain-English Summary:
The buyer pays a base amount, then adjusts it for (i) actual cash on the balance sheet, (ii) actual funded indebtedness, and (iii) a working capital true-up versus a fixed target. A $1.95m portion is carved out into an escrow account (still part of the purchase price economics, but held back to support indemnity claims).

Definition 3: Indebtedness / Debt

Definition Name: “Indebtedness”

Section Reference: Article I (Definitions), Section 1.1, Page 5

Full Verbatim Text:

```text
“Indebtedness” as applied to any Person means (without duplication) all indebtedness of such Person for borrowed money, whether current or
funded, or secured or unsecured, including (a) all obligations of such Person evidenced by bonds, debentures, notes or other similar instruments or debt
securities, (b) all indebtedness of such Person created or arising under any conditional sale or other title retention agreement with respect to property
acquired by such Person (even though the rights and remedies of the seller or lender under such agreement in the event of default are limited to
repossession or sale of such property), (c) all indebtedness of such Person secured by a purchase money security interest or other Lien to secure all or
part of the purchase price of the property subject to such Lien, (d) all obligations under leases that shall have been or must be, in accordance with
GAAP, recorded as capital leases in respect of which such Person is liable as lessee, (e) any liability of such Person in respect of banker’s acceptances
or letters of credit, (f) all interest, fees, prepayment premiums and other expenses owed with respect to the indebtedness referred to above and (g) all
indebtedness referred to above that is directly or indirectly guaranteed by such Person or that such Person has agreed (contingently or otherwise) to
purchase or otherwise acquire or in respect of which it has otherwise assured a creditor against loss.
```

Plain-English Summary:
Indebtedness is defined primarily as borrowed money and debt-like obligations, including capital leases, letters of credit, and all related interest/fees and prepayment premiums, plus guarantees. It does not explicitly include several items that often appear in broader buyer-friendly definitions (e.g., bank overdrafts, intercompany debt, operating lease liabilities, factoring).

Part 22: Suggested Revisions

Revision 1: Clarify Escrow as Holdback From Seller Proceeds
Current Language:
“plus (c) $1,950,000 (the ‘Escrow Amount’)…” (Section 2.1, p.10)
Issue: Drafting can be read as incremental consideration; conflicts with funds-flow sentence in 3.3(b).
Suggested Revision:
Add: “The Escrow Amount shall be withheld from amounts otherwise payable to Seller at Closing and deposited with the Escrow Agent pursuant to the Escrow Agreement.”
Rationale: Removes ambiguity; aligns with escrow agreement recital that it is “withheld.”
Likely Pushback: Minimal; mostly drafting clarification.
Fallback Position: Add a closing funds flow schedule stating exact wire amounts (seller net proceeds, lenders, escrow).

Revision 2: Add Transaction Expenses Definition and Economic Routing
Current Language: No “Transaction Expenses” concept in 2.1 or definitions.
Issue: Potential leakage/dispute; company-paid deal costs might not be captured.
Suggested Revision:
Define “Transaction Expenses” and subtract them from Aggregate Purchase Price (or explicitly include/exclude them from Current Liabilities).
Rationale: Aligns economics and reduces disputes.
Likely Pushback: Seller will argue it’s already priced and/or captured in WC.
Fallback Position: Keep no separate price deduction but add explicit inclusion in Current Liabilities (buyer-favorable) or explicit exclusion plus requirement Seller pays outside Company (seller-favorable).

Revision 3: Route Overdrafts Clearly
Current Language: “Cash… (which amount may be negative)” (p.3); Indebtedness does not mention overdrafts (p.5).
Issue: High risk of double count/under count.
Suggested Revision:
“Cash shall not be less than zero; any overdrafts or negative balances shall be included in Indebtedness.”
Rationale: Market clarity.
Likely Pushback: If Seller expects overdrafts, they may resist debt treatment.
Fallback Position: Explicitly treat overdrafts as negative Cash and exclude from Indebtedness.

Revision 4: Align Escrow Claim Deadline With Survival
Current Language: Survival 15 months (9.1, p.31) vs escrow claim deadline/release May 8, 2008 (Escrow 2(d)/(g), pp.41–42).
Issue: Potential gap where indemnity exists but escrow released.
Suggested Revision:
Extend escrow claim deadline/release to match Survival Date, or provide that Seller remains obligated to pay valid claims up to cap after escrow release.
Rationale: Align remedy security to survival.
Likely Pushback: Seller wants escrow released earlier.
Fallback Position: Keep earlier release but require Seller guarantee/LOC for remaining survival tail.

Revision 5: Broaden Closing Indebtedness Scope
Current Language: “Indebtedness… funded and outstanding” (2.1, p.10).
Issue: Potentially excludes debt-like items.
Suggested Revision:
Define Closing Indebtedness to include overdrafts, cash pooling, intercompany debt to Seller/affiliates, and any debt payoff fees/premiums.
Rationale: Align to typical “net debt” capture.
Likely Pushback: Seller argues it inflates deductions.
Fallback Position: Enumerate only the specific known facilities (schedule-based).
