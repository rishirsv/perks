Part 2: Financial Definitions

Important framing for Parts 2–7
This document is a **public-company style merger agreement** with **fixed per-share cash consideration**. As a result, standard private-company SPA “purchase price adjustment” definitions (Cash, Indebtedness, Working Capital, Transaction Expenses, Net Debt, accounting hierarchy for true-up) are largely **not used** and therefore **not defined**. Where those definitions are missing, I flag them as “NOT FOUND” and explain what is typically expected in a completion-accounts SPA.

Definition 1: Purchase Price / Consideration

Definition Name: “Merger Consideration”

Section Reference: Section **2.1(b)**, PDF p. **11**

Full Verbatim Text:

```text
(b) each share of Common Stock outstanding immediately prior to the Effective Time (other than Dissenting Shares or Cancelled Shares) shall be
cancelled and converted into the right to receive $11.00 in cash, without interest (the “Merger Consideration”).
```

Part 3: Purchase Price Mechanics

3.1 Price Equation

Because this is a fixed-price merger (not a completion accounts SPA), the “purchase price” is best reconstructed as the aggregate of cash payments due at (or following) the Effective Time under Article II.

Reconstructed formula (aggregate consideration mechanics)

[Base Purchase Price]
= **Merger Consideration** per share × **Eligible Shares**
Where:

- Merger Consideration = **$11.00 cash per share** (Section 2.1(b), PDF p. 11)
- Eligible Shares = Shares outstanding immediately prior to Effective Time **excluding** Cancelled Shares and Dissenting Shares (Sections 2.1(a)–(b), 2.5, PDF pp. 11, 13)

* [Warrant component]
  = **Warrant Consideration** per warrant × warrants outstanding
  Where: Warrant Consideration = (Merger Consideration − exercise price, if any excess) × shares underlying (Section 2.2, PDF p. 11)

* [Equity award cash-out at Effective Time]
  = **Cash paid for Company LTI Awards** (Pre-2023 time-based RSUs + Pre-2023 performance-based RSUs + Pre-2023 performance cash awards) (Section 2.4(b)–(d) and 2.4(f), PDF pp. 11–13)

= [Closing payment pool funded into Paying Agent]
Parent deposits cash sufficient to make aggregate payments required at Effective Time pursuant to Article II (Section 2.6(a), PDF p. 13–14)

Part 22: Suggested Revisions

Revision 1: Strengthen seller remedy if financing fails without requiring debt financing already funded

Current Language (excerpt):

```text
...the Company shall be entitled to seek and obtain specific performance ... only in the event ... (iv) the Debt Financing ... has been funded or will be funded at the Closing...
```

(Section 8.12(b), PDF p. 84)

Issue:
This condition can make specific performance practically unavailable if debt financing is not already funded/ready, even if Parent is otherwise obligated to close. It shifts significant deal-completion risk to the Company, with remedies pushed into a capped fee framework.

Suggested Revision:
“...provided that the Company may seek specific performance to cause Parent to consummate the Closing if all conditions to Parent’s obligation to close are satisfied (other than those that by their nature are to be satisfied at Closing) and Parent has access to equity financing sufficient to fund the Merger Consideration, with debt financing availability to be addressed through Parent’s obligation to arrange financing in accordance with its covenants.”

Rationale:
Aligns enforceability with buyer obligation and avoids a circular “debt must be funded before we can force you to close” outcome.

Likely Pushback:
Buyer will argue lenders cannot be forced and equity-only closing may be impossible.

Fallback Position:
Increase the Parent Termination Fee tier applicable to financing failure, or broaden “Retained Claims” under the Limited Guarantee.

Revision 2: Increase clarity around debt instruments covered by payoff mechanics

Current Language (excerpt):

```text
...obtain... payoff letters... for... Credit Agreement Payoff Amount... and ... Indenture Redemption Amount...
```

(Section 5.17, PDF p. 62)

Issue:
If there are other debt-like obligations (e.g., leases, hedges, guarantees) that must be dealt with for closing, this clause may not flush them out.

Suggested Revision:
Add a schedule listing all material indebtedness requiring payoff, release, or consent at closing, with responsible party and timing.

Rationale:
Reduces closing risk and improves financing sources/uses accuracy.

Likely Pushback:
Company may resist expanded disclosure or schedule burden.

Fallback Position:
Deliver lender payoff list and release requirements as a closing deliverable condition.

Revision 3: Address unclaimed funds mechanics more seller-friendly

Current Language:

```text
Any portion of the funds ... that remains unclaimed ... twelve (12) months ... shall be paid... to Parent...
```

(Section 2.6(f), PDF p. 14–15)

Issue:
Stockholders who miss the 12-month window lose access to the paying agent pool and must pursue Surviving Company without interest, which may be practically harder.

Suggested Revision:
Extend the paying agent holding period (e.g., 24 months) and require continued availability of a claim process through Parent or Paying Agent.

Rationale:
Improves holder experience and reduces reputational risk.

Likely Pushback:
Buyer prefers shorter paying agent liability.

Fallback Position:
Enhanced notice/communication obligations and clear claim procedures post-12 months.
