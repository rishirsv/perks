Full Verbatim Text:

```text
“Cash” means, with respect to the Company, all bank deposits and other cash that may be fully withdrawn on demand and
cash equivalents, in each case, determined as of immediately prior to the Closing; provided, that to the extent not taken into account in
```

Part 3: Purchase Price Mechanics

3.1 Price Equation

Based on Section 2.4 (Closing Consideration), the closing-time equation is:

Base Consideration ($50,000,000)

- (Estimated Cash – Estimated Closing Indebtedness), but only “the amount, if any, by which Estimated Cash exceeds Estimated Closing Indebtedness”
  +/- (Estimated Net Working Capital – Target Net Working Capital ($850,000))
  – Estimated Transaction Expenses
  – Escrow Amount ($750,000 total; comprised of $500,000 adjustment escrow + $250,000 indemnity escrow)
  = Closing Consideration payable at Closing (with escrow withheld and deposited to escrow accounts)

Section Reference: Section 2.4 (PDF p. 21 / Agreement p. 17)

Verbatim Formula Language:

```text

agrees to sell to Purchaser, and Purchaser hereby agrees to purchase from each Seller, all of such Seller’s Shares for an amount equal
to the Closing Consideration, payable at the Closing. The “Closing Consideration” shall consist of (i) the Base Consideration, plus
(ii) the amount, if any, by which Estimated Cash exceeds Estimated Closing Indebtedness, plus or minus, as the case may be, (iii) the

Transaction Expenses, minus (v) the Escrow Amount.
```

9.2 Metric Definition

Verbatim Definition (Qualified Revenue):

```text
“Qualified Revenue” means revenue from the sale of the products and services of the Company, in each case, determined in
accordance with GAAP applied on a basis consistent with the Company’s historical accounting practices and methodology (but solely to
the extent such practices and methodology are consistent with GAAP), without duplication, net of (a) any write-offs, discounts, rebates,
refunds, and returns, (b) any sales, use, value-added or other applicable Taxes, and (c) any amounts billed as revenue to a customer or
similar party which are past due for a period of more than ninety (90) days after such amount is billed or that are otherwise being
contested or are subject to a claim of set-off by the customer or similar party. For the avoidance of doubt, Qualified Revenue for any
period shall include (i) any amounts paid to the Company by Exclusive Customers (including amounts paid by Exclusive Customers for
commuter products and services), and (ii) any amounts paid to the Company by the Purchaser’s customers (excluding any Exclusive
Customers) sourced by the Company.
```

(Definition, PDF p. 16 / Agreement p. 12)

Departures from GAAP/standard definition:

- Excludes aged/contested receivables (>90 days past due, contested, or subject to set-off) even if GAAP revenue recognition might still record revenue depending on collectability estimates.
- Adds a customer-source concept (“Purchaser’s customers … sourced by the Company”) that is not a GAAP concept and will require tracking rules.

Adjustments/add-backs:

- Net of write-offs/discounts/rebates/refunds/returns and taxes.
- Excludes aged/contested receivables.
- Includes all amounts paid by “Exclusive Customers,” including commuter products/services.
- Includes amounts from purchaser customers sourced by company (excluding Exclusive Customers).

Part 22: Suggested Revisions

Revision 1: Clarify net cash/debt sign convention
Current Language:
“plus (ii) the amount, if any, by which Estimated Cash exceeds Estimated Closing Indebtedness…” (Section 2.4, PDF p. 21 / Agreement p. 17)
Issue: Could be read as only upward adjustment when cash > debt, not downward when debt > cash.
Suggested Revision:
Replace clause (ii) with: “plus (ii) Estimated Cash minus Estimated Closing Indebtedness (which amount may be positive or negative).”
Rationale: Eliminates asymmetry and dispute risk.
Likely Pushback: “Intent is clear.”
Fallback: Add interpretive sentence: “For the avoidance of doubt, if Estimated Closing Indebtedness exceeds Estimated Cash, Closing Consideration shall be reduced by such excess.”

Revision 2: Narrow Indebtedness litigation and deferred revenue categories
Current Language:
“(b) all liabilities with respect to past or present litigation…” and “(k) any long-term deferred revenue.” (Indebtedness definition, PDF p. 11 / Agreement p. 7)
Issue: Material buyer-favorable value shift; potential double count and uncertainty.
Suggested Revision:
Limit litigation to “accrued, booked amounts reflected on the Closing Balance Sheet” and move deferred revenue to NWC.
Rationale: Aligns with market treatment of operating liabilities and working capital economics.
Likely Pushback: Buyer wants protection against under-reserved liabilities.
Fallback: Require a specific schedule listing the included litigation accruals and deferred revenue balances as “Closing Indebtedness” items.

Revision 3: Fix earnout drafting cross-reference and add attribution methodology
Current Language: Section 2.12(a) references Section 2.12(g) which does not exist. (PDF p. 25 / Agreement p. 21)
Issue: Drafting defect and ambiguity.
Suggested Revision: Correct cross-reference; add a schedule defining “sourced by the Company” with objective rules and reporting evidence.
Rationale: Reduces disputes and strengthens enforceability.
Likely Pushback: Operational burden.
Fallback: Implement a joint committee or Audit Firm interpretive authority on sourcing.
