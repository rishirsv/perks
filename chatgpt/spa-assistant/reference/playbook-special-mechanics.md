# Playbook — Special Mechanics

## Earn-outs / reverse earn-outs [earnouts_reverse_earnouts]

Plain-English meaning  
Earn-outs pay additional (or reverse) consideration based on post-closing performance. The real economics come from the accounting basis, operational covenants, and dispute process—not just the headline metric.

Typical / Buyer-leaning / Seller-leaning patterns

- Typical: defined metric, measurement period, earn-out statement, dispute to independent accountant.
- Buyer-leaning: operational flexibility, exclusions/adjustments, ability to net/offset (with guardrails).
- Seller-leaning: consistent accounting principles, no purchase accounting, audit rights, and limited offsets.

Pitfalls + drafting tells

- Metric is defined but accounting basis is not locked (reclassification risk).
- No-hindsight / no-reclass protections missing.
- Offsets/set-offs are broad and can swallow the earn-out.
- Dispute expert scope is not limited to disputed items.

Counsel questions

- What Accounting Principles apply to the earn-out statement (and what controls in a conflict)?
- Are purchase accounting/step-up effects excluded unless expressly intended?
- What operational covenants apply (and what is prohibited/required)?
- Are offsets permitted, and if so, only for finally determined amounts and through what mechanism?

Likely pushback

- “Buyer needs flexibility to operate the business.”

Rebuttals / fallback

- “Flexibility is fine with objective accounting rules and a narrow list of carve-outs.”
- “Offsets should be limited and procedural to prevent unilateral netting.”

Minimal edits

- The Earn-Out Statement shall be prepared ~~in Buyer’s discretion~~ **in accordance with the Accounting Principles and consistently applied**.
- For certainty, the earn-out metric shall be calculated ~~including purchase accounting adjustments~~ **excluding any purchase accounting or step-up adjustments**.
- The Independent Accountant shall resolve ~~any matters it deems appropriate~~ **only the disputed line items set forth in the Earn-Out Dispute Notice**.

SPA evidence anchors

- Project North — earn-out defined terms (Interac Earn-Out Amount; PPJV Earn-Out Amount).
- Simply Solventless — reverse earn-out package (Reverse Earnout Amount/Period/Statement/Note).

## Equity awards / options / phantom equity [equity_awards_options_phantom]

Plain-English meaning  
Equity awards mechanics determine whether optionholders/warrantholders are paid out, rolled over, or canceled—and whether any reserves are effectively a hidden escrow.

Typical / Buyer-leaning / Seller-leaning patterns

- Typical: cash-out in-the-money; cancel out-of-the-money; appoint paying agent; reserve for withholding.
- Buyer-leaning: reserve can cover withholding/claims and is released only after remittance/clearance.
- Seller-leaning: narrow reserve purpose, capped and short duration; more immediate release.

Pitfalls + drafting tells

- Award payments treated as Cash adjustment / Transaction Expenses (double count or misclassification).
- Reserve account operates like escrow with no release/dispute mechanics.
- Withholding/payroll tax mechanics are unclear or over-broad.

Counsel questions

- Are awards cashed out or rolled over, and which awards?
- Is any reserve limited to statutory withholding/remittance, and is it time-limited?
- Are award payments treated as part of consideration (not as a Cash or WC adjustment item)?
- Who controls the paying agent and release instructions?

Likely pushback

- “We need a large reserve; payroll is messy.”

Rebuttals / fallback

- “Reserve can exist but must be capped, purpose-limited, and released on objective triggers.”
- “Keep award payouts out of Cash/WC adjustments to avoid double counting.”

Minimal edits

- The Reserve Account shall be used ~~for any claims Buyer asserts~~ **solely for statutory withholding and remittance obligations arising from the Cash-Out Options**.
- Any remaining reserve balance shall be released to holders ~~at Buyer’s discretion~~ **within [__] days after all applicable remittances are made**.
- Cash-Out Options payments shall be treated as ~~Transaction Expenses~~ **part of consideration payable to holders and shall not reduce Closing Cash or Net Working Capital**.

SPA evidence anchors

- KeyMe — Cash-Out Options defined terms and Section 3.1(d).
- Project Eleos — Phantom Equity Award Amount.
- Simply Solventless — SSC Warrant.
- Vortex — Option / Option Plan constructs.

## Locked box / leakage / ticker [locked_box_leakage_ticker]

Plain-English meaning  
Locked box fixes the price using historical accounts; leakage rules prevent value transfer between the locked box date and Closing. Ticker/interest can compensate for time value.

Typical / Buyer-leaning / Seller-leaning patterns

- Typical: define leakage, permitted leakage schedule, claim process, interest on leakage, expert determination.
- Buyer-leaning: narrow permitted leakage; longer claim period; clear interest/ticker calculation.
- Seller-leaning: broader permitted leakage schedule; caps; shorter claim window.

Pitfalls + drafting tells

- Permitted Leakage schedule is not exhaustive.
- Leakage overlaps with Taxes/Transaction Expenses without “without duplication.”
- Interest/ticker definitions are vague or inconsistent.
- Expert roles (leakage vs consideration) are unclear.

Counsel questions

- Is Permitted Leakage exhaustive and scheduled?
- How are tax-related leakages treated (and is there overlap control)?
- What is the interest/ticker formula and period?
- Who decides disputes and is the expert scope limited to disputed items?

Likely pushback

- “The schedule is sufficient; we don’t need more.”

Rebuttals / fallback

- “Make the schedule expressly exhaustive; otherwise the parties will litigate what was ‘permitted’.”
- “Add a no-duplication sentence so leakage isn’t recovered twice.”

Minimal edits

- “Permitted Leakage” means ~~any payments disclosed to Buyer~~ **only the items expressly listed in Schedule [Permitted Leakage]**.
- Buyer may recover Leakage ~~in addition to any other adjustment~~ **only once, and no amount shall be recovered both as Leakage and as a Transaction Expense or Tax item (without duplication)**.
- Leakage Interest shall accrue at ~~a reasonable rate~~ **[__]% per annum from the Locked Box Date to the Closing Time**.

SPA evidence anchors

- Vortex — Locked Box Date/Accounts; Leakage; Permitted Leakage; Known Leakage Amount; Leakage Interest; Ticker Amount; Leakage Expert.

## Consortium / subscription constructs [consortium_subscription_constructs]

Plain-English meaning  
In consortium/subscription structures, part of the economics may be paid via subscriptions while another leg purchases shares. Enterprise Value and allocation mechanics can move value between legs if not tightly drafted.

Typical / Buyer-leaning / Seller-leaning patterns

- Typical: headline Enterprise Value and subscription prices, plus proportional share mechanics and sometimes separate closing times.
- Buyer-leaning: clear allocation rules, no double adjustment of EV, bridge for value drift between closing times.
- Seller-leaning: flexible allocation, limited bridging, and broader permitted value movements.

Pitfalls + drafting tells

- Enterprise Value is adjusted in multiple places (silent double count).
- Purchase Closing Time and Subscription Closing Time differ with no economic bridge.
- Waterfall/rounding mechanics shift value between legs.

Counsel questions

- Is Enterprise Value headline-only or an adjustment input (and where else is it adjusted)?
- Are there multiple closing times, and who bears value drift between them?
- How is Purchase Price Proportionate Share calculated and documented?
- Are rounding and allocation mechanics stated (and who benefits)?

Likely pushback

- “These are investor-side mechanics, not purchase price mechanics.”

Rebuttals / fallback

- “They directly change cash flows and allocation; the SPA must prevent double adjustment and allocate drift.”
- “Keep it short: define EV’s role, define allocation math, define who bears drift.”

Minimal edits

- Enterprise Value shall be ~~adjusted for Net Debt and Working Capital~~ **a headline amount and shall not be adjusted except as expressly provided in Section [Purchase Price]**.
- Any value transfers between Purchase Closing Time and Subscription Closing Time shall be ~~ignored~~ **treated as Leakage (or deducted from consideration) unless expressly permitted**.
- “Purchase Price Proportionate Share” means ~~an amount determined by Buyer~~ **Enterprise Value multiplied by the applicable ownership percentage, as set forth in Schedule [__]**.

SPA evidence anchors

- Project Skyrocket — Established Enterprise Value; Aggregate Subscription Price; Ares Subscription Price; CDPQ Subscription Price; Purchase Price Proportionate Share; Purchase Closing Time vs Subscription Closing Time.
- KeyMe — Minority Interest Amount.
