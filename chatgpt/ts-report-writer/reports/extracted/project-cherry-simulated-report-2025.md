# Quality of Earnings
## Overview
- The quality of earnings analysis is prepared based on monthly trial balances, audited/reviewed financial statements, and discussions with management and advisors.
- The nature of adjustments includes VDD adjustments, potential adjustments, and other considerations.

## Quality of earnings adjustments
- 1. Captive insurance: Adds back optional uninsurable coverage purchases that began in FY18 and removes the related premiums.
- 2. Leachate disposal costs: Adds back historical disposal and hauling costs; management advised those costs are no longer expected after installation of a self-contained leachate disposal process.
- 3. Closing of branches: Adds a branch consolidation run-rate adjustment related to Charlevoix and Gaylord.
- 4. Wexford costs: Adds back expensed costs associated with the development of a leachate disposal well (later reversed in potential adjustments).
- 5. Containers expense: Adds back expensed container additions under the reported policy threshold.
- 6. Gain/loss on sale of assets: Removes non-operational gains/losses from EBITDA; includes one-time gains from service vehicle dispositions.
- 7. Reversal of Wexford costs adjustment: Reverses the initial Wexford adjustment when capitalized treatment was confirmed.
- 8. Wexford landfill recapture mechanism: Removes over-accrued expenses and out-of-period recovery related to landfill perpetual care obligations.
- 9. Closing of branches (partial reversal): Removes or revises the initial branch consolidation adjustment and replaces it with alternative assumptions.
- 10. Officer compensation expense: Normalizes owner officer compensation by removing distributions and replacing with normalized salaries.
- 11. Officer compensation & labor adjustments: Removes salaries and benefits for individuals not expected to be part of the ongoing operations and grosses up payroll amounts.
- 12. Personal expenses: Removes owner personal expenses that were recorded on company cards.
- 13. Life insurance: Removes discretionary life insurance coverage for management.
- 14. Hazar-Bestos profits annualized: Removes annualized profits related to 2018 HBC acquisition from EBITDA bridge, with alternative offset in revenue.
- 15. IFRS leases: Adds back lease expense associated with right-of-use assets to align with client policy where applicable.
- 16. Bad debts: Removes historical charge and adjusts to actual bad debt write-offs.
- 17. Workers’ compensation year-end adjustments: Adjusts historical year-end accrual treatment.
- 18. One-time incentive compensation / audit fees / run-rate adjustments: Removes non-recurring or subjective items, including historical controller and support team transitions and potential third-party fee normalizations.
