# Key Findings
## Financial due diligence
- TTM Jan-19 reported EBITDA of $7.1 million increased to $8.4 million after Management and potential adjustments.
- TTM Jan-19 potentially adjusted EBITDA decreased to $7.0 million after pro forma adjustments.
- Significant Management adjustments to TTM Jan-19 EBITDA include:
- 2018 Bonuses paid in 2019 (increase of $0.1 million): Represents an adjustment to move bonuses paid in 2019 relating to 2018 performance to their appropriate period.
- Duplicate CFO costs - salary (increase of $40k): During the transition to a new CFO, there was an overlap during which salaries were paid to both the new and old CFO. The adjustment removes the duplication of costs.
- Potential and pro forma adjustments increased TTM Jan-19 average NWC from $(2.2) million reported to $(1.7) million.
- Significant potential adjustments identified during diligence to net debt include:
- Non-operational items: Adjustment adds back income taxes payable and lease obligations to net debt.
- Revenue increased from $11.5 million in FY17 to $36.8 million in TTM Jan-19, primarily driven by the customer Velicept, which contributed $13.7 million in FY18, and $23.9 million in TTM Jan-19.
- Velicept represented 66.4% of the total sales in TTM Dec-18, which increased from 61.3% in FY18. As a whole, Top ten clients represented 90.9% of total sales from TTM Dec-18.
- Non-Velicept revenue declined from $10.6 million in FY17 to $8.7 million in FY18. This subsequently increased to $11.8 million in TTM Dec-18.
- Actual revenue from new customers in FY18 and TTM Jan-19 was partially offset by customers with declining trends, which typically were due to gaps between clinical trials.
- For customers signed at the start of the period, actual revenue exceeded forecast revenue in FY18 and T11M Dec-18, with positive variances of $14.5 million, and $8.7 million respectively.
- In FY18 and T11M Dec-18, Velicept primarily drove the positive variance between actual and forecasted signed revenues.
- Total wages have increased from $4.5 million in FY17 to $7.8 million in YTD Dec-18A.
- Travel costs have increased from $1.3 million to $1.8 million in FY17 to TTM Jan-19, respectively.
