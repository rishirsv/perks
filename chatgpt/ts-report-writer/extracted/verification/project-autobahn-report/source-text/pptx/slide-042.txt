Scientific Research and Experimental Development (“SR&ED”) (1)
Supporting Analysis
Risk of ITC reduction under a detailed CRA review
A detailed review can be either a technical or financial review and typically 20% of all SR&ED claims submitted to CRA annually are selected for detailed review. Target’s FY17 SR&ED claim has been selected for detailed review by CRA.
A detailed technical review would involve a review of the science behind the project being claimed. During this type of review, CRA will generally review both the technical eligibility of the submission and the eligibility of the associated costs. In the event of a detailed technical review, there is a risk that significant portions of the claim could be denied because under a detailed technical review the entire project submitted could be disallowed.
A detailed financial review only generally involves a review of the appropriateness of the associated costs. In the event of a detailed financial review, the risk of a reduction to the ITCs claimed is generally much lower than a detailed technical review as only the ineligible expenditures of a project are disallowed.
Risk of technical ineligibility
Over the past few years we have noted that CRA has become increasingly stringent during technical reviews and has adopted a narrower view of the types of activities that qualify for SR&ED tax credits.
Scientific Research and Experimental Development (“SR&ED”)
Canada has a SR&ED tax incentive regime which provides tax benefits to corporations carrying out qualifying activities (i.e., “systematic investigation or research carried out in a field of science or technology by means of experiment, analysis or modification”).
Federal incentives
Federal ITCs are available for eligible CCPCs at a refundable enhanced rate of 35% on (up to C$3 million) of eligible expenditures versus a non-refundable rate of 15% that is available to non-CCPCs (and CCPCs with eligible expenditures above C$3 million). ITCs are included in taxable income in the year following the year in which the ITC was received.
The expenditure limit for refundable Federal ITCs at the reduced rate may be reduced where a corporation’s aggregate amount of taxable capital and taxable income exceed certain thresholds.
Provincial (Ontario) incentives
i. Ontario innovation tax credit (“OITC”)
Provincial OITCs are available for all corporations with a permanent establishment (“PE”) in Ontario at a refundable rate of 8% on up to C$3 million of eligible expenditures. OITCs are included in taxable income in the year following the year received.
Similar to federal incentives, the expenditure limit may be reduced where a corporation’s aggregate amount of taxable capital and taxable income exceed certain thresholds.
ii. Ontario research and development tax credit (“ORDTC”)
Provincial ORDTCs are available for all Canadian corporations with a PE in Ontario at a non-refundable rate of 3.5% of eligible expenditures.
