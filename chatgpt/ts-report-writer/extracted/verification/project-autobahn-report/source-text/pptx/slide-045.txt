GST/HST – related party transactions (cont’d)
Management
represented that Target paid expenses related to a corporate jet owned by a related party,
Thinkworks
and now J2ASM Air, and received a reimbursement for these expenses (set off against amounts owed to these companies for travel, on a per diem basis). The reimbursement for the expenses are likely not to be considered to be payments in respect of a taxable supply. Further, to the extent Target claimed input tax credits for these expenses, they could be denied on the basis that they were not incurred in the course of its commercial activities (it was the paying agent, and did not own or lease the jet) and were incurred in the course of
Thinkworks
or J2ASM’s commercial activities (the principal). If the expenses were Target’s responsibility under arrangements between the jet owner and Target, the
IPCs
are likely to be appropriate (e.g., HST on fuel costs related to Target’s travels
).
PST
Management represented that Target is not registered for PST in BC, MB or SK or QST in Quebec. Based on Management representations that Target is not carrying on business in QC, BC, MB or SK (it has no presence in those provinces) and not making sales to customers in BC, MB or SK, it should not be required to register as a vendor for PST purposes in those provinces or for QST in Quebec.
GST/HST
Management represented that Target is registered for GST/HST.
Management represented that Target is collecting GST/HST on all sales except those to non-residents of Canada. It represented that the majority of the sales are to non-residents of Canada, with 5
%-10%
of customers being in Canada (2-3 Canadian customers).
Management represented that it treats its supplies as services rather than software or intangible personal property. It is likely that Target is providing supplies of intangible property, or both.
Generally, supplies of services and of software or intangible personal property to non-residents of Canada are zero-rated for GST/HST purposes, subject to certain exceptions. The exceptions likely do not apply. Supplies of software or intangible personal property to non-residents are not zero-rated if the customer is GST/HST-registered. Management represented that it uses address to determine residency, and that they confirm whether the customer is GST/HST-registered. If the customers are non-residents of Canada under the provisions of the Excise Tax Act and they are not registered for GST/HST purposes, GST/HST should not apply to those supplies to non-residents.
For Canadian sales, management represented that it charges GST/HST based on the address of the customer. This should be appropriate based on the place of supply rules.
Management confirmed that invoices for which Target claims
IPCs
are in the name of Target or a duly authorized agent.
GST/HST – related party transactions
Management represented that it does not charge GST/HST on related party transactions and no election (section 156) has been filed. Target could be liable for uncollected GST/HST plus arrears interest on taxable supplies made to related parties. The general reassessment period is 4-years.

approximately C$9.7M from
related parties; however, we understand that
these amounts relate
to
loans,
which are exempt financial services. Based on information provided, there does not appear to be any taxable supplies made by Target to related parties.
