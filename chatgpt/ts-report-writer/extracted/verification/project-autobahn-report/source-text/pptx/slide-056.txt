ASC 606 – Impact assessment
Application guidance – Contract
costs
Narrative
Current
accounting
Axiom provides sales commissions to employees based on a percentage of the invoiced amounts to the customer
. If the employee leaves the company, no further payments are required.
Revenues also include pass through revenues related to reimbursements from the client for incurred expenses. These are presented on a gross basis.
Requirements of ASC 606
An entity capitalizes incremental costs to obtain a contract with a customer, e.g. sales commissions, if it expects to recover those costs.
However, if these
commissions contain an additional
substantive performance requirement, such costs are not capitalized. A contract acquisition asset is not recognized if the entity does not incur a liability (or obligation) when the contract is approved because future service is required by the commission recipient to earn the payment.
Entities performing services for customers often incur out-of-pocket
costs as part of delivering that service, such as travel. In these situations, the entity incurs such costs in fulfilling the entity’s performance obligation. Regardless of whether a customer imposes restrictions or limits on its reimbursement of out-of-pocket costs, typical out-of-pocket costs and the reimbursements thereof from the customer should be presented on a gross basis.
Implications of adoption
The sales commissions that Axiom pays to its sales reps
contain a substantive performance requirement, i.e. remaining employed during the contract term. Therefore, in effect, there is a ‘double trigger’ applicable for these commissions. Hence, the costs are not incremental costs of obtaining a contract.
Typically,
Axiom’s contracts appear to include pass through costs in the total budget. These costs are invoiced based on actual spending.
These costs relate
to Axiom’s costs to deliver services, as opposed to the customer’s costs. Therefore, the costs are accounted for in accordance with the fulfilment costs guidance (typically expensed as incurred) and the revenues are treated as a variable consideration (and therefore allocated to the performance obligations). This is in accordance with the current accounting treatment of Axiom.
