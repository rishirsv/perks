Requirements of ASC 606
For performance obligations recognized over time, as a practical expedient, if an entity has a right to invoice a customer at an amount that corresponds with the value to the customer of the entity’s performance to date, the entity can elect to recognize revenue at that amount.
Implications of adoption
It is likely that Axiom may not be able to apply the ‘as invoiced’ practical expedient under ASC 606. This is because typically, a significant amount of the ‘Think/Deploy’ revenues are invoiced upfront, prior to goods or services being provided to the customer. In addition, if management concludes that some of the promises included in the ‘Think/Deploy’ phase are related to set-up activities, these invoiced revenues also do not directly relate to goods or services that have been transferred to the customer. Hence, the practical expedient would not be applicable.
Key judgement areas
If management believes that the invoiced value corresponds with the value of the goods or services provided to the customer, Axiom would be able to apply the practical expedient. However, in our experience, the criteria to apply the practical expedient likely would not be met unless changes were made to current invoicing practices.
