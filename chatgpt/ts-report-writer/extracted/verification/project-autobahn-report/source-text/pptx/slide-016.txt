Permanent establishment
Management and Tax Advisor represented that 90%-95% of Target’s total sales were to US-based customers, and Target engages 5 US-based individuals on a full-time basis. Management represented Target pays these individuals through a professional employment organization that collects and pay taxes for each employee based on the states where they live. Tax Advisor clarified that these individuals are not on the payroll of Axiom.
A company may create a PE through the activities of a person acting on the company’s behalf in a foreign country.  A PE generally exists if employees or other dependent agents in the source country have authority to conclude sales contracts in the company’s name. If a taxpayer has a PE in a foreign country, it could be subject to filing requirements and incur tax liabilities. Management represented that two of the US-based individuals act as relationship personnel to US-based clients, and these individuals do not maintain the right to conclude contracts and all contracts are negotiated and executed in Canada. Management represented the other three individuals do not deliver any services in the US as well. As a result, Management represented Target has no permanent establishment in the US.
On this basis that Management and Tax Advisor represented that Target has no US employees, no contracts are ever signed or approved in the US, and no services are delivered in the US or to US customers, and no US permanent establishment exists for Target, Target has never registered or filed any income or indirect US tax compliance filings. It appears reasonable to conclude that no US filings are warranted. Although there is a low risk of a potential tax liability or cash tax exposure in the US, we recommend that Axiom file treaty exempt returns for historical periods and going-forward.
