ASC 606 – Impact assessment
Step 2 – Identify performance
obligations (1/2)
Narrative
Current
accounting
Axiom’s contracts contain
detailed budget calculations and schedules that outline numerous activities to be performed. These activities are bucketed into ‘Think/Deploy’ and ‘Launch’ revenue streams for which revenues are tracked separately. Revenue is recognized as these activities are performed based on the stated price for the activity as outlined in the contract.
Requirements of ASC 606
Under ASC 606 an entity
is required to identify the promised goods and services in the contract and then determine which are separate
performance obligations (i.e. the unit of account).
A performance obligation is a promised good or service that is distinct or a series of distinct goods or services that are substantially the same.
Set-up activities, as opposed to promised services, provide no incremental benefit to the customer beyond that which they will receive from access to the hosted application. In other words, set-up activities represent those tasks that are necessary for the customer to begin to use and benefit from the hosted application.
Implications of adoption
In
Axiom’s contracts, numerous activities are explicitly mentioned. The potential impact for Axiom will depend on which goods or services (together) are distinct and the extent to which certain activities are determined to be set-up activities. Currently, the promises included in the ‘Think’ and ‘Deploy’ revenues together and ‘Launch’ revenues are recognized separately, which implies the identification of two revenue elements. Based on discussion with management, the following might be identified as performance obligations under ASC 606:
Performance obligation 1: Implementation services
‘Think’ and ‘Deploy’ goods and services relate to the implementation and set-up of the goods and services promised in the ‘Launch’ bucket, such as project planning, configuration, customization, data migration and other consulting services. In discussion with management, they indicted that the various activities in this phase provide incremental benefit to the customer beyond that which they will receive from simply receiving access to the hosted application, i.e. they are not set-up or administrative activities. They also indicted that the promises in this phase are all considered to relate to implementation services and that there would be a significant integration service between the activities. In this case, the multiple promises in the ‘Think/Deploy’ phase would be considered a single performance obligation. Some additional considerations in this area are included on the next page.
Performance obligation 2: Managed services / data management
‘Launch’ activities relate to granting access to the hosted application and to providing ongoing project and data management services. The promises in this phase are all considered to relate to services provided over the study operational period. Significant integration of these activities is typically provided and therefore the multiple promises in this phase would be considered to be a single performance obligation.
Performance obligation 3: Training services (optional)
In both the ‘Think/Deploy’ and the ‘Launch’ phase training services are often included. The price charged for the training is based on standard hourly rates. However, trainings are often optionally included in the contract and will only be provided if the customer requests these services. If the training is optional and the price is based on standard hourly rates, the training services are not a performance obligation at the start of the contract. Instead, a separate contract arises once the Customer requests the training.
