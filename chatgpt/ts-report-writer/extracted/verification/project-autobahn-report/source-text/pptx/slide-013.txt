Key analysis
Scope limitation
The scope of our findings are based on discussions with Management and Tax Advisor and information as provided in the VDR by Management (See Appendix 9). To date, we have requested, but have not received, certain information as outlined in Appendix 9. Consequently, our scope has been limited and further tax exposures could exist.
Tax Advisor represented that they are solely engaged with income tax compliance work with respect to Target. We have not performed any formal tax diligence in foreign tax jurisdictions in respect of the US, India, and Europe.
We recommend consulting legal counsel regarding obtaining comprehensive representations, warranties, and an appropriate indemnity sufficiently broad to cover all potential Canadian tax exposures or all open taxation years. The representations, warranties and indemnity should survive the period for which the tax returns can be reassessed.

Related party loan receivables
Based on our read of the YTD19 financials, Target has approximately $C9.7M of loan receivables due from related party parties. Management represented all loans are non-interest bearing. Based on our review of the FY19 related party loans balance and Management representation, we understand that a portion of the balance will be settled prior to closing, but Target is expected to have outstanding loan receivables post-closing. Management represented the outstanding balances due from
Thinkworks
and J2ASM will continue to be amortized post-closing, and any remaining balance will be settled at the next exit of Target.
We have estimated that Target will have approximately C$5.0M ($3.9M) of loan receivables outstanding at closing. We recommend confirming the mechanics of the settlement of all loan receivables with legal counsel, as the Proposed Transaction assumes the business will be debt free at close based on the LOI. We also recommend confirming in the SPA that all loan receivables will be settled at their fair value.
Pre-closing dividends
Management represented that Target will declare an eligible dividend to Andrew Schachter for approximately C$189,000 prior to closing. Based on our review of Target’s FY18 tax return, Target appears to have sufficient attributes to pay the eligible dividend.

SR&ED – CCPC status
As the Buyer is a non-resident, on the earlier of the signing of the share purchase agreement or binding letter of intent, where Buyer obtains the right to acquire more than 50% of the shares of Target, Target will lose its CCPC status. This will impact future SR&ED claims such that the Federal and Ontario tax credit amounts will no longer be refundable (i.e., they will only be available to offset  income tax payable).
Post-closing, Target will be entitled to Federal SR&ED ITC credits at a 15% rate of eligible expenditures. Since FY16, Target has not been eligible for an enhanced rate of ITCs applicable to certain qualifying CCPC’s with the exception of FY18 due to a one-time tax adjustment. Management represented that Target is anticipated to have larger SR&ED claims in FY19 and onward. Accordingly, Target will be subject to the 15% rate for ITCs as a result of the loss of CCPC status post-closing.
