Revenue trends
Revenue increased from $11.5 million in FY17 to $36.8 million in TTM Jan-19, primarily driven by the customer Velicept, which contributed $13.7 million in FY18, and $23.9 million in TTM Jan-19.
Velicept represented 66.4% of the total sales in TTM Dec-18, which increased from 61.3% in FY18. As a whole, Top ten clients represented 90.9% of total sales from TTM Dec-18. You may wish to consider the risks associated with customer concentration in your assessment of the deal.

Operating expenses
Total wages have increased from $4.5 million in FY17 to $7.8 million in YTD Dec-18A. Average headcount has been steadily growing, rising from 57 in FY17 to 98 in YTD Dec-18A. This was partially driven by the Company converting subcontractors into employees, which Management represents was done to reduce overall costs.
From FY17 to FY18, headcount of full-time employees increased by 47.5%, while sub-contractor headcount decreased by 51.0%.
Travel costs have increased from $1.3 million to $1.8 million in FY17 to TTM Jan-19, respectively. This is primarily driven by the costs associated with the private jet.
The private jet was also used by other related parties. In the historical period analyzed, the entirety of costs associated with the usage and maintenance of the private jet are borne by Axiom. Subsequently, Axiom allocates the usage not associated with the Company, and increases a related party receivable. Upon completion of the proposed transaction, our understanding is this structure will not exist, as Axiom will only be charged for its direct proportionate usage of private jet costs.
