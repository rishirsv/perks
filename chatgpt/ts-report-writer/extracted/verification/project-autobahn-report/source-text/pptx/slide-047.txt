High level impact assessment
We performed a high level impact assessment of the adoption of ASC 606 by Axiom. Our observations are based on the readily available materials, such as the audited financial statements, sample contracts, interview with management and other materials that were provided by Axiom. Our impact assessment indicates areas that are likely to be impacted by ASC 606 for revenue recognition upon adoption of the standard. It is noted that additional assessment considerations could be identified upon implementation of the new revenue recognition standard. In our experience, in the implementation phase more details and facts and circumstances are typically identified, because a larger sample of contracts will need to be reviewed and more corroborative inquiry (for example with operations personnel) may lead to new insights and newly identified impact areas. Below the main impacted areas that were identified in the impact assessment are included.

Multiple deliverable accounting
Currently,
Axiom recognizes revenue based on specific activities that are bucketed into two categories ‘Think/Deploy’ and ‘Launch’. Under ASC 606, this current split of revenues may not be aligned with the identification of performance obligations, which will affect both the timing and amount of revenue recognized. Particularly, if some of the activities related to ‘Think/Deploy’ are determined to be set-up activities (e.g. activation activities or set-up of user interfaces), fees related to these activities will need to be deferred and recognized as part of other performance obligations.
n
Determining stand-alone selling prices
For each performance obligation identified, the stand-alone selling price will need to be determined. Management represented that the stated contract prices are considered to be an appropriate basis to determine stand-alone selling prices. However, evidence to support this statement likely may require implementing a new process. In addition, the level (i.e. the grouping of activities) at which stand-alone selling price is estimated is directly affected by the determination of performance obligations discussed above.

We note that the above potential impact areas are judgemental. Depending on the application of judgement and evaluation of the facts and circumstances of individual contracts, it is possible that the Company may arrive at similar revenue recognition to their current accounting. Conversely, if changes to the accounting are required, the amount of the impact likely would vary from one contract to another and therefore is very difficult to accurately estimate or extrapolate.
