Target’s SR&ED claims
The SR&ED eligible work activities for which the ITCs are granted have to meet a strict CRA criteria, known as a 5 part eligibility test. Based on the content of project descriptions (form T661, Part 2) for fiscal FY16
and FY17 the
two SR&ED projects claimed do not pass the eligibility test. More specifically, a) there does not appear to be evidence of identification of scientific or technological uncertainties (TUs). The TUs mentioned in the report do not appear to be technological in nature and there is little evidence of due-diligence activities performed to confirm existing knowledge, methods, tools, frameworks etc. could not be used to overcome the TUs; b) the work performed generally does not support ‘systematic investigation or search’ and appears to be routine engineering/development, using known methods, tools, knowledge etc.; and c) the technological advancements (TAs) mentioned are do not appear technological in nature; they do not convey an advancement in the underlying area of science or technology.
The technical project description (“PD”) for FY17 is virtually identical to that of FY16. The particular concern is the content of the work claimed for which eligible costs are identified appear identical for the two fiscal periods. This is generally unacceptable to the CRA and may result in complete denial of the FY17 SR&ED claim if the CRA was to compare the claim to the FY16 submission.
Given that fiscal
FY17
SR&ED
claim is under review (both technical and financial), there is a significant risk for the
FY16 claim
as well, if the CRA was to go back and compare the two years to establish a correlation between the two years for eligible work performed
. However, based on Management representation, the FY16 claim as been accepted as filed.
Normally, once the SR&ED claims are accepted as filed without a review, the CRA doesn’t go back to re-assess a previously accepted SR&ED claim, unless there is a suspicion of fraud (intended or otherwise).
Based on the content of two
FY16 PDs
, it is very likely the SR&ED claim will be challenged by the CRA; as such, we estimate between
50% to 70%
of the claim may be at risk
.
The target has filed their fiscal 2018 SR&ED claim; typically once a review of the prior period claim is underway, the CRA waits for the review to finish before processing the current year SR&ED claim. Therefore, it is very likely the 2018 SR&ED claim will be reviewed as well, unless the 2017 SR&ED claim is accepted as filed after the review.
