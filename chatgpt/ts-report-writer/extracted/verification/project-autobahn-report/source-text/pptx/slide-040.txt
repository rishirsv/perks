CCPC status
On the earlier of the signing of the share purchase agreement or binding letter of intent, where a buyer (i.e., either a non-resident corporation or a Canadian corporation, that is controlled by a non-resident company) obtains the right to acquire more than 50% of the shares of a Canadian target, the Canadian target may lose its CCPC status.
That is, on the acquisition of the majority of the issued and outstanding shares of the Company, Buyer should have the right to control the Company, such that the Company will be controlled directly or indirectly in any manner whatever by a non-resident. As a result the Company is expected to lose its CCPC status.
Axiom will have a deemed taxation year-end immediately before the time of losing its CCPC status. Axiom will be required to file a corporate tax return for the deemed year-end, due six months following the deemed year-end.
We generally recommend, where otherwise feasible, the SPA is signed on the day of closing to limit the number of deemed year ends.
Where the SPA is signed in advance of closing, Axiom will have two deemed year-ends, that is (i) on the loss of CCPC status and (ii) on the AOC.
FY17 Tax Filing
Based on Target’s financial statements, Target expenses all research costs. Software development costs are capitalized as intangible assets when the Company can demonstrate that the technical feasibility of the project has been established.
Generally in open taxation years, the amounts Target capitalized to intangible assets for accounting purposes overlap for tax purposes and are capitalized to computer software (Class 12) for the capital cost allowance. However, from Target’s FY17 tax return filing, there were significant costs that were capitalized for accounting purposes but did not meet the criteria for capitalization for tax purposes. As a result, Target generated a taxable loss for FY17 as a result of the deduction of approximately C$2M to taxable income.
We have not confirmed the detailed breakdown of the tax deduction but generally, any impact should only be of timing in nature.

When the shares of Target are acquired, there will be an acquisition of control (“AOC”) of Axiom for Canadian income tax purposes. The AOC rules will generally result in a deemed taxation year-end for Axiom. Corporate income tax returns for the deemed year-end will be due within 6 months after the deemed year-end.  Final tax payments will be due 2 months after the deemed year-end.
The AOC rules will generally result in the realization of any inherent losses on certain assets, as well as a write-down of assets to their FMV.  Furthermore, existing NOLs will be restricted post-acquisition; and any previously unutilized capital or property losses will expire.
Under the AOC rules, the use of any NOLs of Target that may arise in FY19 or pre-AOC could be restricted as follows:
The NOLs can only be utilized if the business that incurred the NOLs is carried on for profit throughout the particular year in which the carry forwards are utilized; and
The NOLs can only be applied against income from the business that incurred the carry forwards and from any other business substantially all of the income of which was derived from the sale of similar properties or similar services.
As of FY18, Target has utilized all of its non-capital loss carry forwards.
