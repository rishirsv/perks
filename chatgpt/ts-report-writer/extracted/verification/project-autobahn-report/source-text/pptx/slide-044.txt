Independent Contractors
Supporting Analysis
Contractors in foreign jurisdictions
Independent
contractors status is question of fact for Canadian tax purposes. Factors considered by the CRA in order to determine if any individual is an independent contractor or employee include: degree of control over the worker, tool/equipment used by the worker, subcontracting work by the worker, financial risk taken by the worker, etc.
Where an individual independent contractor is determined to be factually an employee, the employer may be liable for amounts on remuneration not withheld and remitted, including benefits such as CPP, EI, and payroll taxes to the extent that they were under or unpaid by the employee/contractor, in addition to penalties and interest related to its failure to withhold and remit these amounts.
