Requirements of ASC 606
ASC 606 includes specific guidance on the treatment of contracts for the license of intellectual property (‘IP’). A
software license is considered to be provided only if the following criteria are met: (1) the customer had the contractual right to take possession of the software at any time without a significant penalty, and (2) it is feasible for the customer to either run the software on its own hardware or
contract with another party unrelated to the entity to host the software
.
Implications of adoption
Customers of Axiom do not have the contractual right to take possession of the software. Rather they are only granted access to the hosted application.
In case a contract is terminated, the customer has no option to obtain the software. The underlying data of the customer is provided in a certain offline format.
The criteria for an arrangement to include a software license are not met and therefore the contracts should be accounting for as service arrangements under the general ASC 606 model.
