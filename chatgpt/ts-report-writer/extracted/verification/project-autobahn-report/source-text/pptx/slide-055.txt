Requirements of ASC 606
The transaction price allocated to a performance obligation is recognized when (or as) the performance obligation is satisfied,
i.e. when (or as) the customers obtains control of the promised goods or services.
Revenue is recognized over time if one of the three criteria for over time recognition is met: (1) the customer simultaneously receives and consumes the benefits provided by the entity’s performance as the entity performs, (2) the entity’s performance creates or enhances an asset that the customer controls as the asset is created or enhanced, or (3) the entity’s performance does not create an asset with an alternative use to the entity, and the entity has an enforceable right to payment for performance completed to date. If none of these criteria is met, the revenues are recognized at the point in time at which control of the good or service transfers to the customer.
When an entity charges a consistent per transaction or per usage fee, the variable fees can be allocated entirely to the period in which they are earned if the criteria of the variable consideration allocation guidance (refer to step 4) are met. This is because the changes amounts reflect changes in the value to the customer, i.e. the value to the customer of its access to the managed services correlates with how much the customer makes use of the managed services.
Implications of adoption
Typically
implementation services and managed services / data management
will meet the
requirements to be recognized over time. The measure of progress used to recognize revenue over time will need to depict Axiom’s performance. For ‘Think/Deploy’ phase this could potentially be based on hours of work completed or costs incurred. This could potentially lead to a slightly different revenue recognition within the period the ‘Think/Deploy’ activities are performed. The potential impact is not likely to be significant given the short timeframe these activities typically entail (i.e. approx. 3 months).
For managed services, the most appropriate measure of progress is most likely based on usage or ratable recognition over time. This means that the revenues are recognized in the period in which the services are provided, consistent with Axiom’s current policy.
Key judgement areas
In selecting a method of measuring progress for implementation services, management should consider the nature of the services and choose a measure that depicts the Company’s performance. However, given the
short timeframe in which implementation services are provided and that revenue is currently recognized based on the underlying activities performed to date, changing the measure of progress related to the ‘Think/Deploy’ phase is not expected not lead to any significant changes in revenue recognition.
