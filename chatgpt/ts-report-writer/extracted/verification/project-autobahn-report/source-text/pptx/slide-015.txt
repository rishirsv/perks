Indirect tax – input tax credits
Management represented that Target paid expenses related to a corporate jet owned by a related party,
Thinkworks
and now J2ASM Air, and received a reimbursement for these expenses (set off against amounts owed to these companies for travel, on a per diem basis). The reimbursement for the expenses are likely not to be considered to be payments in respect of a taxable supply. However, to the extent Target claimed input tax credits for these expenses, there is a low to medium risk such input tax credits may be denied in the event of a CRA assessment on the basis the costs were not incurred in the course of Target’s commercial activities (as Target was the paying agent, and did not own or lease the jet), and were incurred in the course of
Thinkworks
or commercial activities.
If the expenses were Target’s responsibility under arrangements between the jet owner and Target, the IPCs are likely to be appropriate (e.g., HST on fuel costs related to Target’s travels). We recommend conducting further tax due diligence to confirm whether Target claims any input tax credits in respect of costs related to
Thinkworks
’ commercial activities.

Indirect
tax – related party transactions
Management represented that it has historically not charged any GST/HST on related party transactions and no related party sales tax exemption elections has been filed. As such, Target could be liable for any uncollected GST/HST plus arrears interest on taxable supplies made to related parties in historical periods.
As of December 2019, Target has loan receivables from J2ASM of C$2.1M of and from
Thinkworks
of C$7.4M. Management represented that the receivable from J2ASM is amortized by the rental payments due from Target to J2ASM on a monthly basis. Similarly from FY17-FY18, the receivable due from
Thinkworks
is reduced by Target’s proportionate share of travel costs in connection the company jet owned by
Thinkworks
up until November 2018. Management represented that as of November 2018, J2ASM Air was incorporated and took over the ownership and financed a new lease for the aircraft, and the arrangement with Target subsequently ceased.
As the transactions described above relates to loans, these should be exempt financial services for GST/HST. Based on information provided, there does not appear to be any taxable supplies made by Target to related parties. Management represented Target would file the relevant sales tax exemption elections between related parties post-closing. We recommend that post-closing, Target file the appropriate sales tax elections and review its indirect tax compliance withholding obligations with J2ASM and J2ASM Air going-forward.
