Financial

function
There were various financial processes and procedures identified during due diligence that may need to be improved under DWHP’s ownership.
The Company’s finance and accounting team is comprised of an accounting manager and several clerks located at the Company’s head office in New Jersey, USA.  Additional employees supporting the bookkeeping, accounts payable, accounts receivable, and payroll processing functions are located in India.
The
Company’s finance department has historically not included a CFO and/or a Controller.
In December 2017, the Company hired an external consultant to support the contemplated transaction.
The individual is tasked with the responsibilities of an interim CFO.
Historically,
the Company’s financial reporting encompassed:
“Soft” monthly closing procedures and relied on the assistance from their auditors for year end closing procedures. However, beginning in January 2017, the Company began to improve the monthly close process.
No monthly accruals. During the 2016 year end, Company’s auditors quantified the accrued revenue and payroll amounts. These amounts were booked by the Company however were not subsequently reversed until December 2017.
No amortization of annual prepaids. In December 2016, the Company set up prepaid assets for various items including insurance. Management did not amortize these prepaid assets on a monthly basis.
No reconciliation of intercompany balances on a monthly basis.
In addition
to as noted above, the interim CFO is mandated with the responsibility of improving the overall financial reporting process of the Company.
