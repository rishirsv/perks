# Business Overview
- American Waste, Inc. and Affiliates (the "Company") is comprised of a vertically integrated solid waste business ("American Waste") and an environmental and industrial services business ("Northern A-1").
- American Waste provides collection, processing, disposal, and recycling of solid waste in Northern Michigan to 100,000+ residential and commercial customers.
- Northern A-1 offers environmental and waste disposal solutions to industrial markets across 20 states with nine strategically located injection wells.
- The Company was founded as American Waste in 2002 and merged with the previously founded Northern A-1 in 2003.
- In 2010, the Company acquired Northeastern Exploration ("NEE"), which provides a class I injection well, and acquired Hazar-Bestos Corporation ("HBC") in 2018, which provides asbestos remediation capabilities.
- It is our understanding that GFL Environmental Inc. is evaluating the acquisition of American Waste, Inc., excluding Aircraft operations and the Excel Site Rentals, LLC (50% equity ownership) entity, referred to as the transaction perimeter or the "Business".

# Quality of Earnings

## Quality of earnings adjustments
1. Captive insurance:
   - This adjustment adds back optional uninsurable coverage purchased by the Company starting FY18 as Management represented that it is discretionary and not required of the buyer.
2. Leachate disposal costs:
   - In FY18, the Company invested in a self-contained leachate disposal well.
   - This adjustment adds back the historical disposal and hauling costs, which are not expected to be incurred as a result of the new investment.
3. Closing of branches:
   - Management represented that the Company plans to close and consolidate hauling operations from Charlevoix and Gaylord into a new facility in Elmira, MI, in order to gain operating and cost efficiencies.
4. Wexford costs:
   - Management indicated that subsequent to further discussion, all costs related to the development of the self-contained leachate disposal process were capitalized, and VDD adjustment #4 was reversed.
5. One-time expenses:
   - Adjustment removes payments to a company affiliated with the owners, professional fees related to the potential sale of the business and excess / personal charitable donations.
6. Labor adjustments:
   - Adjustment adds back the salary of three individuals who do not play a role in the business, including the owners' family members and those employed for personal home maintenance and repairs.
7. Personal expenses:
   - Adjustment eliminates owner personal expenses placed on the Company credit card.
8. Officer compensation expense:
   - This adjustment removes the officer compensation recorded, including distributions, and replaces it with normalized officer salaries of $160,000 each for two officers.
9. Life insurance:
   - Adjustment removes the officers' life insurance coverage for Mike Ascione (CEO) and Eddie Ascione (COO) as it was discretionary and not required post-acquisition.
10. Hazar-Bestos profits annualized:
   - The adjustment represents 1/12th of the 2018 profits reported for the acquired entity in order to annualize the impact of the acquisition in FY18.
11. Containers expense:
   - This adjustment adds back the container expenses for items under $5,000 in order to align with the Client's accounting policy of capitalizing such items.
12. Gain / loss on sale of assets:
   - This adjustment removes gains and losses on the sale of fixed assets from EBITDA as they were not related to the core operations of the business.
13. Reversal of Wexford costs adjustment:
   - As such, VDD adjustment #4 was reversed.
14. Wexford landfill recapture mechanism:
   - This adjustment removes the out of period recovery in November 2018 and adjusts the accruals across the historical period to nil.
15. Closing of branches (partial reversal):
   - We have proposed an alternative adjustment based on reduced facility and headcount expenses.
16. Other adjustments:
   - Officer compensation and labor adjustments.
   - Run rate finance department headcount.
   - Employee health insurance expense.
   - Workers' compensation year-end adjustments.
   - Audit fees.
17. IFRS leases.
18. Bad debts.
