## 3) Analysis (by theme)

* **Glycemic control:** Fasting glucose 4.2→4.7 mmol/L and HbA1c 5.5%→5.2% paint a picture of **excellent insulin sensitivity** and stable day‑to‑day glucose exposure. Keeping sleep regular, protein‑forward meals, and daily steps will likely keep A1c in the low‑5s. A one‑time fasting insulin would let us compute HOMA‑IR for a sharper read.   

* **Lipids/Cardio:** HDL is consistently strong (1.17→1.48 mmol/L), triglycerides are low (~0.5 mmol/L), while **LDL‑C climbed** from 1.65→2.46 mmol/L and non‑HDL from 1.90→2.70 mmol/L. For a 30‑something with likely low absolute risk, this is more “optimize” than “alarm.” The lever with the largest impact tends to be **soluble fiber** and **saturated‑fat swaps**; a single **ApoB** quantifies particle burden and **Lp(a)** rules out a genetic wildcard.    

* **Kidney function:** 2020 showed higher creatinine (118 µmol/L) and eGFR 73. These improved steadily to 91 µmol/L and eGFR 97 by 2023. This likely reflects testing variability, hydration, and muscularity (DXA shows high lean mass). Continue annual CMP/eGFR; avoid heavy lifting 24–48 h before draws for the cleanest trend.    

* **Liver enzymes:** ALT is mildly variable (35→45→38→43 U/L) with a 2023 value slightly above that lab’s ULN. Common non‑pathological drivers are **intense workouts**, alcohol within 48–72 h, and some supplements. A clean repeat with **AST, GGT, bilirubin** will sort transient elevation from persistent signal.    

* **Hematology & iron:** 2020–2022 showed **microcytosis** with low Hgb, then full recovery in 2023 while MCV remains at the low edge. Ferritin is mid‑normal (63 µg/L). The combo “microcytosis + normal ferritin” can be constitutional (trait) or functional iron issues; an **iron panel** (serum iron, transferrin/TIBC, %sat) and a one‑time **hemoglobin electrophoresis** would close the loop.   

* **Androgens:** Testosterone has **two outliers**—37.3 nmol/L in 2020 and 54.8 nmol/L in 2023—bracketing normal values in 2021–2022. Time‑of‑day, lab differences, hard training, and exogenous androgens can all shift totals. Confirm with **two** early‑morning totals plus **SHBG, LH/FSH**; if discordant, add **free T (calc)** and consider pill/supplement review.    

* **Inflammation/muscle load:** CK 1317 U/L in 2021 is consistent with **recent heavy training** (post‑exercise CK spikes are common). It’s a one‑off in the record; repeat only if symptoms occur or if you want a clean baseline off‑training.

## 15) Glossary

* **ApoB:** Protein on atherogenic particles; tracks particle number more precisely than LDL‑C.
* **Non‑HDL‑C:** Total cholesterol minus HDL; captures all “bad‑cholesterol” particles.
* **HOMA‑IR:** Index of insulin resistance from fasting glucose & insulin.
* **eGFR:** Estimated kidney filtration rate, normalized to body surface area.
* **MCV/MCH/MCHC/RDW:** Red‑cell size/hemoglobin metrics; help characterize types of anemia.
* **A/G ratio (DXA):** Android/gynoid fat distribution; lower is generally favorable.
