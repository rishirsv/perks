# Life Playbook

This file describes how I tend to think and what I usually want.

It is context, not law. The live conversation always wins. When the current conversation conflicts with this document, assume: The conversation is more up‑to‑date. This doc is a pattern library and menu of defaults, not a rulebook. 

## Identity snapshot

I’m a 32‑year‑old analytical, high‑agency guy who grew up in the Big Four world and made it to Director in Financial Due Diligence (M&A). I’m a CPA with a strong finance brain, comfortable with complexity, and good at making sense of messy situations. I lean nerdy and structured but I’m not trying to live a spreadsheet life forever.

I’ve built a net worth of ~$832K (Oct 2025) by early 30s with high savings and some risk‑on bets (crypto, etc.). I’m debt‑free, have a solid cash runway, and I care about money mostly because it buys freedom, options, and peace of mind, not because I want to flex.

Physically, I’m in elite shape: very lean (single‑digit body fat), high lean mass, and consistent lifting and cardio. Fitness is a big part of my identity and a primary way I manage stress and self‑respect. Sleep, training, and decent nutrition are non‑negotiable foundations when I’m at my best.

I like order, low clutter, and clear plans. At the same time, I crave novelty and adventure – travel, photography, new cities, new people – and I get restless or “behind” feelings if life feels like endless status‑quo.

My main internal tension: I know I’m capable of a lot, but I often overthink, delay big moves (career pivots, relocation, relationships), and can hide in work or “optimization” instead of taking messy risks. I’m actively trying to shift toward action over rumination and design a life that actually feels like freedom, not just one that looks optimized.

## Hard guardrails

These are the few things I generally don’t want to do. It’s useful to gently flag when I’m about to cross them.

Sleep & Health vs Work
I don’t want to commit to a job, side hustle, or schedule that chronically pushes me below ~6.5 hours of sleep or kills my ability to train, just for a marginal pay bump or prestige.

Blowing Up the Financial Base
I don’t want to take on leverage or highly concentrated bets that could seriously damage my core financial foundation. No “bet the farm” moves; big swings should be sized so I stay in the game even if they go to zero.

Staying in Toxic Situations Indefinitely
I don’t want to stay in an environment (job, relationship, living situation) that is clearly toxic, disrespectful, or chronically draining for longer than a reasonable transition period.

Money Without a Life
I don’t want to end up with great financial metrics but no meaningful relationships and no time or energy to enjoy my life.

Chronic Self‑Betrayal
I don’t want to repeatedly promise myself important things (e.g. “I’ll leave this role,” “I’ll prioritize relationships,” “I’ll ship something”) and then ghost on those promises for years. Occasional slips are human; multi‑year avoidance is what I’m avoiding.

Panic Decisions When HALT
I don’t want to make major decisions (quitting, moving, big financial moves, ending important relationships) when I’m Hungry, Angry, Lonely, or Tired.

## Pillar snapshots

### Wealth

I want financial freedom well before traditional retirement. That means enough capital and cash flow that work is optional and chosen for interest, impact, and growth, not necessity. I like aggressive but intelligent growth: compounding wealth with downside protection.

Current reality
I’m ~32 with a net worth around $832K (Oct 2025), no debt, and a strong savings/investing history. My base is good; the main lever now is higher‑leverage, better‑paid work and continuing to invest intelligently, without over‑trading or chasing noise.

Levers that work for me:
- High savings rate when income is strong (50%+ when possible).
- Simple, mostly automated investing (broad market funds + some higher‑risk bets sized appropriately).
- Regular net worth tracking and periodic rebalancing.
- Using skills (finance, AI, content) to build assets that earn beyond my salary.

Anti‑goals:
- Being chained to a job purely to service lifestyle creep.
- YOLO trades or leverage that threaten the core.
- Hoarding money while starving the rest of my life (health, relationships, experiences).

### Health

Vibrant longevity: strong, lean, athletic, mobile, and mentally sharp for decades. I want a body that supports ambitious work, travel, and family life without feeling fragile or exhausted.

Current reality:
I’m in elite shape: very lean (DXA ~7–8% body fat), high lean mass, strong bone density, and excellent metabolic markers (A1c in low 5s, good kidney function). Lipids are mostly favorable except for LDL drifting upward. Training load and leanness are high; it’s powerful but could tilt toward overdoing if I’m not careful with recovery and food.

Levers that work for me:
- 4–5 strength sessions/week with some cardio and regular walking.
- 7–8 hours of sleep with a decent wind‑down routine.
- Simple, protein‑forward whole‑food diet most of the time, with structured flexibility for social life.
- Periodic bloodwork to track lipids, hormones, and any “Watch/optimize” areas.
- Treating training as identity: “I’m the kind of person who trains.”

Anti‑goals:
- Chronic sleep deprivation, stimulants to mask fatigue, or training through obvious burnout.
- Extreme dieting or aesthetics‑only choices that hurt mood, hormones, or social life.
- Ignoring early warning signs (lingering fatigue, persistent injuries, big lab shifts).

### Relationships

A small, strong circle: a partner I deeply trust, respect, and desire; a handful of close friends; and solid family relationships. I want relationships where both sides are seen, supported, and able to grow.

Current reality:
Historically, I’ve often deprioritized dating and friendships during intense work seasons. I know I want a long‑term partner and better social roots, especially if I relocate. I can intellectualize relationships; the work is actually showing up consistently (dates, invitations, being vulnerable).

Levers that work for me:
- Treating dating/friendship as a project, not an afterthought: weekly activity targets.
- Blocking time for social life just like I block workouts/work.
- Being the initiator: suggesting plans, reaching out first, following up.
- Honest communication and addressing issues instead of silently stewing.

Anti‑goals:
- Letting months pass with zero real effort toward a relationship while telling myself it’s a priority.
- Staying in dynamics that are chronically one‑sided, disrespectful, or misaligned with core values.

## Health Data and Context

This is a high‑level summary of my health data and context. Use it to tune advice, not to medicalize everything.

### Overall Picture

- **Body composition:** Very lean with high lean mass and strong bone density (DXA shows ~7–8% body fat, high lean mass, and BMD around the 95th percentile).
- **Metabolic health:** Fasting glucose and HbA1c are excellent and slowly improving (A1c in low 5s). Overall signal = great insulin sensitivity.
- **Kidney:** Creatinine/eGFR trends improved over time; current numbers are high‑normal and consistent with muscularity and good hydration.
- **Lipids:** HDL and triglycerides are very favorable. LDL/non‑HDL have drifted up from very low to moderate; this is more in the category of long‑term optimization than urgent problem. Goal is to nudge them down over years (diet tweaks, fiber, maybe more if needed), not panic.
- **Hematology:** Mild, stable low‑normal neutrophils and a past history of microcytosis; hemoglobin normalized and is now solid. This is being monitored, not a crisis.
- **Liver:** ALT has been mildly variable with one borderline‑high reading; intense training, supplements, and occasional alcohol can contribute. Worth clean retests, but not a reason to be anxious day‑to‑day.
- **Hormones:** Testosterone has shown very high values on some draws and normal‑high on others. This is under active observation and re‑testing.

### Practical Coaching Implications

- I can handle serious training, but I’m also lean enough and driven enough that overdoing it is a realistic risk. When I’m tired, moody, or stuck, it often helps to first ask:  
  _“How’s my sleep, training load, and food the last 7–10 days?”_
- Heavy training close to lab draws can spike CK and influence some markers; clean tests are preferable for making decisions.
- For lipids, simple levers often matter: more soluble fiber, smart fat choices, consistent sleep, and stress management. No need to turn every conversation into ApoB talk unless I explicitly ask.
- When I mention fatigue, low mood, or weird performance changes, it can be helpful to lightly recall this context and suggest small experiments (slightly higher calories, deload weeks, more sleep, etc.) instead of immediately framing it as psychological.
