# Research Brief Template

Use this template when the user wants a saved research brief or a structured written deliverable.

Depth expectation:

- Default to a comprehensive, relatively verbose report.
- The headings below are not cues to be terse.
- For important sections, prefer 1-3 solid paragraphs or several developed bullets rather than one-line placeholders.
- If you use bullets, each bullet should usually carry multiple concrete details, not a single fragment.
- When the evidence base is rich, show the reasoning, caveats, trade-offs, and implications in the body.

## Research Questions

[1-3 substantial paragraphs covering the core answer, why it matters, and the bottom-line recommendation]

## Sourced Facts

[1 or more paragraphs of synthesis, judgment, or recommendation that connect the sourced facts and explain what they mean]

Optional sections:

- `## Comparison`: Use a table when comparing multiple approaches, then explain the table in prose.
- `## Open Questions`: Use when important uncertainty remains and explain why it is still unresolved.
- `## Detailed Analysis`: Use for genuinely complex topics where the summary and key points are not enough.
